# The Second Stamp

**Type:** Illegal mutual-repair fellowship — part guild-in-exile, part congregation that doesn't know it's one
**Based in:** The Chimneys of [Kaldrum](../../locations/kaldrum.md), with benches in every Forgeholm work-town
**Alignment:** Ally in potential — unknown to players at campaign start
**See also:** [Forgeholm](../kingdoms/forgeholm.md) · [Religion — the Guest Customs](../religion.md#the-guest-customs) · [The Wayfair](the-wayfair.md)

## What they are

Every certified work in Forgeholm bears two maker's stamps: the wright's own, and beside it a blank die that marks nothing — required by Guild regulation, explained by none ([the Guest customs](../religion.md#the-guest-customs)). Generations ago, somewhere in the punishment rolls, menders doing uncertified repairs began **inking the blank stamp** — pressing the empty die in black, a mark that says *made for someone*, signed by no one the Guild recognizes.

The habit became a language, the language became a fellowship. The Second Stamp is the network of struck-off wrights, unlicensed menders, and Unmarked operators who keep the Chimneys and the work-towns running where the Guild's queues don't reach: the pump fixed in the night, the lift-brake nobody billed for, the apprentice taught out of a tool-apron instead of a certification hall. Every such repair carries the inked blank square, and everyone below the mid-terraces knows what it means. Almost no one on the Bench has ever noticed it exists.

## What they want

**Stated (insofar as they'd state anything):** to fix what's broken for the people it's broken *for*, and be left alone.

**Real (and mostly unexamined):** the Stamp keeps the *why* of work alive in the one kingdom whose Heart drinks exactly that ([the Mother Engine](../anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine)). Their habits — the named repair, the free mend, the inked die — are [Ferrun's real worship](../religion.md#ferrun-the-shaping-hand-forgeholm--technology) surviving as reflex: the purpose spoken over the work, the tool as a promise to the hand that will use it. They are not political. They are about to be.

## How they operate

- **Benches** — cells of three to seven, one senior mender to each, known to each other by stamp and by steam-kitchen. No rolls, no dues; the Ledger of the Retired is the only document the Stamp keeps ([Wrena Calder](../../characters/npcs/wrena-calder.md) holds it).
- **The night queue** — broken things find their way to a bench the way rumors travel; nobody can quite tell you how the queue works, only that it does.
- **The Ledger of the Retired** — the Guild "quietly retires" wrights who ask the wrong questions ([Who Knows What](../anchors-and-engines.md#who-knows-what-gm-reference)); the Stamp has smuggled a number of them out of Forgeholm entirely, several to [the Wayfair](the-wayfair.md) — including, nine years ago, **Ondrel Vasht**.
- **The vent-galleries** — the Stamp maintains what the Guild forgot it built: dead ventilation and haul-ways threading the whole terraced city, including passages within sound of the Root.

## Key Members

- **[Wrena Calder](../../characters/npcs/wrena-calder.md)** — the mender everyone brings broken things to; the closest thing the Stamp has to a center
- **Master Corwith** — the Bench of Masters' youngest member; funds the Stamp through invented maintenance contracts and tells himself it's efficiency
- **Odda Ferr** — lift-crew forewoman in the Chimneys; runs the night queue's northern benches, and is the Stamp member most tempted by [Krane](../../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md)'s offer — her daughter is Unmarked, brilliant, and thirteen

## Relationship to the Party

The party's infrastructure below the Bench's sightlines: safehouses, vent-routes, honest news, and a hundred hands that owe the Stamp a favor. Trust is earned the Chimneys way — by what you fix, not what you say. A party that mends something for nothing, or stands up for the fair when the frame-up lands, gets the inked stamp's protection without ever being told it exists.

## GM Notes

**They are the resistance — one contract from now.** At campaign start the Stamp is pre-political: a repair network, not a movement. Krane's Compact and the frame-up of the fair politicize it in real time, and the party can shape what it becomes: an intelligence network, a saboteurs' cell, the seed of the Guild's reform — or, if Kaldrum falls, the occupation's underground with Wrena at its center.

**The Stamp can split.** Korrven's actual policy — marks for the Unmarked, designed by their own hands — is everything half these people ever wanted, offered sincerely by [a woman from their own terrace](../../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md#gm-notes). Some benches will go over, Odda Ferr's first among the candidates, and they will not be villains for it. Play the split as the Forgeholm arc's version of the campaign's whole question: the difference between a mark that binds you to a king and a repair that binds you to a neighbor is *exactly* the difference between Korrven's community and the real thing.

**The inked die is Amara's mark, unknowing.** The blank stamp is Forgeholm's Guest custom — the space held for the erased sixth ([Religion — GM Notes](../religion.md#gm-notes)). The Stamp has spent a century inking the guest's die on every mended thing: *made for someone* is Love's signature written by people who cannot remember the god. When the party starts assembling the Guest-custom chain, the Second Stamp is the custom that grew *hands*. A Wayfair elder seeing the inked stamp for the first time should go quiet the way they do around Splendor healers ([Magic — GM Notes](../magic.md#gm-notes)).

**Re-tuning role.** When the party attempts the Mother Engine's re-tuning ([what it needs](../anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine)), the Stamp is the native graft-stock: the imported practices (Sophara's *should we*, Agoran's *for whom*) take hold in Forgeholm *through* the Stamp's living habit of the named repair. Mechanically: the ritual needs the vent-galleries to reach the Root, the benches to hold the terraces during it, and Wrena's consecration as the local strand of the braid.
