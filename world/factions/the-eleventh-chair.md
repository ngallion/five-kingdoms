# The Eleventh Chair

**Type:** Exile network and memory-keepers — the old Agoran, scattered and unbroken
**Based in:** The **Downsmoot** in the [Chalklands](../geography.md#the-chalklands-luminar--agoran); the camp on the Stoa's disputed fork; hearths in every kingdom; and, quietest of all, tables inside Agoran itself
**Alignment:** Ally — the party's earliest reachable friends, and the campaign's living archive
**See also:** [Agoran](../kingdoms/agoran.md) · [Oren Brandt](../../characters/npcs/oren-brandt.md) · [Tam Merrival](../../characters/npcs/tam-merrival.md) · [Religion — the Guest Customs](../religion.md#the-guest-customs)

## What they are

The oldest law of the Granary was arithmetic: *a table of ten feeds eleven* — the eleventh place set for the guest, who is whoever comes ([Tavan](../religion.md#tavan-the-long-table-agoran--community)). When the king's grace replaced the old one and the empty chair began to mean *his* seat kept ready, the Agorans who could not swallow it took the only thing that couldn't be garrisoned — the customs — and carried them out through the hills.

The Eleventh Chair is not an army and hates being called a resistance. It is a scattered network of exile hearths — a few thousand souls across five years of flight — that keeps the *old* Agoran alive in working order: the harvest-grace with the right name in it, the naming-days, the table-songs the Hundred Tables no longer sing, the seed-grain of a culture, kept sprouting until it can be planted home. Their recognition sign is the thing itself: the eleventh place laid, bread already broken on the plate — *the guest is expected, and the guest is not the king.*

## Where the hearths burn

- **The Downsmoot** — the main refuge: a ring of turf halls in a Chalklands valley, under the hill-figure of the [six dancers](../geography.md#the-chalklands-luminar--agoran). Neither kingdom claims the downs, so no writ runs there; the exiles graze sheep, cut chalk, and hold a true harvest moot every year — the only one left in the world. [Oren Brandt](../../characters/npcs/oren-brandt.md) keeps its table.
- **The fork island camp** at [the Stoa](../../locations/the-stoa.md) — forty hearths under Hearthmother Bry, landed by [Tam Merrival's Quiet Circuit](../../characters/npcs/tam-merrival.md#backstory). Bry has no patience for names and networks — but her open table *is* the Chair's whole creed in practice, and the Downsmoot considers her camp its southern hearth whether she'd use the word or not.
- **Inside Agoran** — the chair-keepers: households that set the guest's plate the old way and say the old grace behind the shutters. No rolls, no meetings — a membership consisting entirely of a table setting, deniable as habit. Widow Corren's table in [Commonhold](../../locations/commonhold.md#people-here) is the one the party is likeliest to find.
- **Scattered** — exile families in every kingdom's margins, moved by the Quiet Circuit or their own feet, recognizable to each other by the laid place. The Wayfair carries their letters.

## What they want

**Stated:** to keep the table until Agoran can take it back — nothing grander. Feed who comes, remember what was, raise the children knowing the songs.

**Real (and increasingly urgent):** the keepers are aging and the children are exile-born. Every year the old Agoran lives a little more in [Oren's](../../characters/npcs/oren-brandt.md) memory and a little less in practice. The Chair needs what it cannot say aloud: for someone to make going home *possible* before the last moot-speaker dies.

## Relationship to the Party

The best early allies in the campaign: reachable from Tier 1 (exiles among Tam's people, the fork camp, Chair hearths in any kingdom's margins), unarmed and unthreatening, and holding three things the party will need — **the old culture** (living bearers of Agoran's true aspect, required for the [Veritas](../anchors-and-engines.md#veritas--the-silent-heart) and [Forgeholm](../anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine) re-tunings, and the counter-grace for the climax), **the roads** (drovers who know every path into Agoran the Musterfields don't watch), and **the first crack** (see GM Notes). What they ask in return is never fighting. It is *sitting down* — the party that eats at the eleventh place, kingdom after kingdom, is being sworn into something older than it knows.

## GM Notes

**The drovers found the crack.** Old Chair drovers, comparing notes across five kingdoms' margins, noticed years ago that the Hollowing never touches Agoran — they keep a rough map of it, charcoal on a barn wall at the Downsmoot, and have stopped telling people because nobody believes exiles with a grievance. It is the earliest available thread of [the herding proof](../the-hollowing.md#gm-notes): not evidence yet, but the *question*, handed to the party over supper as early as Tier 1.

**The split families.** Nearly every Chair hearth has people at the king's tables — sons in grey cloaks, sisters among [the Found](the-found.md), a daughter who stayed because staying was staying *home*. The Fall did not sort Agoran into good and bad; it sorted it into those who could bear the new warmth and those who couldn't. Liberation scenes should be full of reunions, and none of them simple. (Widow Corren's warden sons; [Iveth Kall and Oren Brandt](../../characters/npcs/iveth-kall.md#gm-notes) are the arc-carrying case.)

**The sixth dancer.** The Downsmoot sits under the one image in the world that still shows six figures holding hands ([Geography](../geography.md#the-chalklands-luminar--agoran)) — and the Chair's whole practice (the laid place, the expected guest, bread already broken) is Agoran's [Guest custom](../religion.md#the-guest-customs) kept *warm* instead of fossilized. They are, unknowing, the closest thing to Amara's congregation outside the Wayfair; an elder of the fair visiting the Downsmoot should go very quiet. When the party assembles the Guest-custom chain, the Chair is where Agoran's link lives.

**Infiltration pressure.** The quiet corps wants what Maro was sent for at the Stoa — the pipeline mapped, the hearths named ([his orders](../../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md#backstory)). The Chair's defense is its formlessness (no rolls, no oaths, a table setting), but the Downsmoot is a fixed place, and the year the king finally considers the exiles worth the trouble is a season the party may have to stand in front of. If the Downsmoot burns, the old Agoran survives only in whoever has learned the songs — make sure, by then, that includes the party.

**The return.** The Chair's endgame is one night: the exiles walking back into Commonhold — not as an army, as *guests* — and the eleventh place laid at the Ring's head table with the whole city watching what the king does about it. Stage it as part of the climax ([Commonhold — GM Notes](../../locations/commonhold.md#gm-notes)); it is the lever that turns liberation from a battle into a homecoming, and it is the Chair's answer to the question the campaign asks about Agoran: what was filled with poison can be re-filled — by the people who kept the recipe.
