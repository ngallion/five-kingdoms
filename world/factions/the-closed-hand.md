# The Closed Hand

**Type:** Movement — part school revolt, part revival, one push from becoming either a renaissance or a mob
**Based in:** The courtyards of [the Stoa](../../locations/the-stoa.md), both banks, with deep roots in the granges
**Alignment:** Ally in potential — and already half-poisoned
**See also:** [Sophara](../kingdoms/sophara.md) · [Religion — Sophar](../religion.md#sophar-the-weigher-sophara--philosophy) · [Maro Tallis](../../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md) · [Kest Varro](../../characters/npcs/kest-varro.md)

## What they are

Old statues of Sophar have one hand open — the question — and one closed: **the answer**. Modern statues have both hands open ([Religion](../religion.md#sophar-the-weigher-sophara--philosophy)). The movement takes its name and its sign from what was lost: a single closed hand, chalked on walls, on lecture-court doors, and — scandalously — over the open second hand of the modern statues themselves.

The Closed Hand is the loose network of censured students, restless young rhetors, grange families, and a few grey heads who demand one thing: **that Sophara answer something.** Resume the docket. Hear the Pending. Render the Verdict of the Fork. It crystallized around [Kest Varro](../../characters/npcs/kest-varro.md)'s censure and swelled the week the camp landed — because the camp turned their abstraction into forty hearths of people the kingdom must answer *about*.

## What they want

**Stated:** verdicts again — the docket resumed, the Pending heard, the scale allowed to come to rest.

**Real (and mostly unexamined):** they are [Sophar's real worship](../religion.md#sophar-the-weigher-sophara--philosophy) reviving — the faith of the Weigher who *answers*, surfacing as politics in a kingdom that forgot it was ever faith. Their chalked hand is the god's forgotten hand. None of them would put it that way. Keeper Ashwel, watching from the temple garden, is beginning to.

## How they operate

- **Circles** — courtyard chapters of five to fifteen, self-organized, coordinated (everyone would say loosely; in fact, almost entirely) by [Maro Tallis](../../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md), who books the courtyards, keeps the accounts, and remembers everyone's name.
- **The chalked hand** — the sign, renewed nightly, scrubbed daily. The Provisional has filed a motion to prohibit it. The motion is in the Pending, position ninety-one thousand and something, which the movement finds hilarious and useful.
- **The Pending readings** — their masterstroke: reading shelved petitions *aloud in public*, oldest first. Two hundred years of unheard questions — a widow's water-rights, a village's plague appeal, a child's letter asking why the roads end — read slowly, by name, to crowds that come back every night and go home very quiet.
- **The camp suppers** — circles increasingly meet at Hearthmother Bry's long table on the island, where Sopharans discover what deciding feels like *(see [the Stoa — GM Notes](../../locations/the-stoa.md#gm-notes))*. The orthodoxy has noticed the correlation without understanding it.

## Key Members

- **[Kest Varro](../../characters/npcs/kest-varro.md)** — the face: the censure that started it, the signature everyone copies, the voice at the readings
- **[Ereni Thole](../../characters/npcs/ereni-thole.md)** — the reluctant grey head: lends her tattered standing, keeps the young ones out of cells, and trusts the movement's luck not at all
- **[Maro Tallis](../../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md)** — the organizer: "an exile of the '09 flight," indispensable, beloved, and the king's
- **Junior Steward Odelle Fasque** — the leak: a clerk of the Pending who copies the docket's index for the readings and has decided, quietly, that tending an archive of ignored suffering is not a neutral act

## Relationship to the Party

The party's natural base in Sophara — hands, crowds, courtyards, and the granges' goodwill — and their most fragile asset: everything the Hand does, it does at the edge of becoming something worse. A party the movement admires steers it by example ([the fight for Kest](../../characters/npcs/kest-varro.md#gm-notes)); a party that ignores it leaves the steering to Maro.

## GM Notes

**The name is the battleground.** A closed hand is an answer given — or a fist raised. The whole arc's question lives in that ambiguity, and Maro's project is to settle it the wrong way: *the answer* becomes *the strong hand*, becomes *someone must*, becomes the drafted motion to accept the king's protection. Track the drift in what the chalk looks like — an open palm closing gently, or knuckles.

**The split, when it comes.** Like [the Second Stamp](the-second-stamp.md), the Hand can break — and its fist-wing will not be villains: they are the people who listened to two hundred years of unheard petitions and concluded, reasonably, that this kingdom's process is beyond saving. Whether the split happens before or after the Verdict — and whether Kest is standing on the near or far side of it — is the arc's barometer, and mostly the party's doing.

**At the Verdict,** the Hand is the crowd, the readings are the overture, and the chalked sign is on every wall between the fairground and the Hall. If the arc goes well, the movement's demand is granted *in the oldest possible form* — the high rite returned, the scale at rest — and the Hand's chalk becomes, without anyone deciding it, the first new liturgy of the Weigher in 213 years.

**Re-tuning role.** Sophara's healing needs Forgeholm's practice and Agoran's accountability present at the Still Heart ([anchors file](../anchors-and-engines.md#sophara--the-still-heart)) — but it needs a *native* graft-stock too, and the Hand is it: their living habit of the question answered in public. Mechanically: the ritual wants the camp's table on the island, a Forgeholm hand at the Stillworks, and the Hand's circles holding the banks — the courtyards concluding, aloud, all night.
