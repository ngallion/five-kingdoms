# The Found

**Type:** The king's movement — part chosen family, part administration, part church of belonging
**Based in:** [Commonhold](../../locations/commonhold.md) and every table in Agoran; wardens and agents abroad in all four free kingdoms
**Alignment:** Enemy — and the campaign's hardest moral terrain
**See also:** [Korrven](../../characters/npcs/the-king.md) · [Agoran](../kingdoms/agoran.md) · [The Hollowing](../the-hollowing.md) · [The Wayfair](the-wayfair.md)

## What they are

Korrven's followers do not call themselves an army, a party, or a faith. They say: *he found us.* The Found began in the margins — the [Thornfell](../geography.md#the-thornfell-agoran--forgeholm) camps, the punishment rolls, the unsorted wilds — as the people no kingdom had a shelf for, gathered one at a time by a man who asked each of them what they would have been if anyone had let them, and then listened to the whole answer. When Agoran welcomed him in 209, the Found became its spine: the stewards, quartermasters, teachers, and wardens of the fallen kingdom, and the most devoted administrative class in the world.

Their creed fits on a cloak: ***No one stands alone.*** They mean it. Every word of the movement's public face is true — the rescues, the grain, the open door, the marks-for-the-Unmarked policy Krane offers in Kaldrum. The Found are not cynics serving a tyrant. They are the saved, serving the man who saved them, and most of them are not bad people. That is the point. Fighting them is not clean.

## How they operate

- **The King's Table** — the inner circle at Commonhold's head table: [Warden-General Bram Harrow](../../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md), [First Steward Iveth Kall](../../characters/npcs/iveth-kall.md), and abroad, [Ilsabet Krane](../../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md), the King's Provisioner. No titles of nobility, no court dress — the Found's leaders serve food at the feasts they run.
- **The King's Wardens** — the public arm abroad: grey-cloaked companies riding to any kingdom's rescue against the rising wilds, free of charge ([The Hollowing](../the-hollowing.md#the-kings-wardens)). Heroes, and most of them believe every word of it. The offer that follows every rescue — a garrison, a beacon-line, a warden-post — is the lattice growing.
- **The quiet corps** — wardens of the interior who keep the king's story clean: minders of the rolls' loose threads, handlers of embedded agents ([Maro Tallis](../../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md) is theirs), readers of everything the Hall of Names hears. Politely invisible inside Agoran; deniable outside it.
- **The Dousers** — the deepest ring, hidden *inside* the warden structure and run through the Survey house's route-orders: the herders who aim the Hollowing by carving channels of cut connection ([the true mechanism](../the-hollowing.md#gm-notes)). A few dozen riders, most of whom understand their work as grim triage — *the tide must go somewhere; better the ledger choose than chance.* Their existence is the movement's mortal secret; most of the Found, including most wardens, have no idea.
- **The recruiters** — the Found grow by finding. Recruiters work every kingdom's margins — the struck-off, the censured, the Grey After's aching crowds — offering exactly what the Wayfair offers, at interest. The one called **the Gleaner** shadows the fair's circuit itself ([the Wayfair — hooks](the-wayfair.md#campaign-hooks)): Tier 1 social adversary, patient and kind, harvesting the fair-ache. *(Sketch stats: Difficulty 11; no real attacks — moves: **The Open Seat** — a lonely NPC ally is offered belonging, now; **Debt of Thanks**, spend a Fear — someone the party helped repays a Found kindness with the party's names.)*

## What they want

**Stated:** shelter for everyone the kingdoms throw away; safety on the roads; no one standing alone, anywhere, ever again.

**Real (and known in full only to the king):** every anchor re-tuned to Korrven, every kingdom bound into a community that cannot exile him ([the endgame](../anchors-and-engines.md#the-endgame--the-keystone-problem)). The rank and file are not lying about the mission — the mission is lying to them.

## Relationship to the Party

The Found will try to recruit the party long before they try to kill them — a company of remarkable, unshelved people from five kingdoms is precisely what the king collects ([Krane's read](../../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md#relationship-to-the-party)). Expect rescues that create debts, offers that solve real problems, and enemies who remember the party's names, feed them, and grieve them. When it comes to blades at last, the party should recognize faces that could have been Wayfarers.

## GM Notes

**The mirror.** The Found and the Wayfarers are the same people sorted by which fire found them first ([the Wayfair — GM Notes](the-wayfair.md#gm-notes)): the lonely who found the fair got a family and a road; the lonely who found the king got a family and an enemy. Many on both sides carry Amaranth's blood ([History — GM Notes](../history.md#gm-notes)) — the heirs of Love, marching under the thing that erased them. Play the symmetry hard, and remember the ending it points at: if the keystone is ever restored, these two populations together are who could rebuild Amaranth.

**How they break.** The Found cannot be beaten in the field without proving the king's story. They break from *inside*, along the seams between the rings: wardens learning what the Dousers are ([Harrow's ledger](../../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md#gm-notes)), stewards learning what the Setting-Out feeds ([Commonhold — GM Notes](../../locations/commonhold.md#gm-notes)), the saved learning what the saving costs. Each reveal should fracture, not shatter: some of the Found will double down (the story is all they have), some will go quiet, and a precious few will do the bravest thing in the setting — leave one family twice.

**Turning them.** Every named member of the Found is reachable in principle, because gratitude is real and can be re-aimed ([the Maro precedent](../../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md#gm-notes)). The cost rule: turning a Found lieutenant should always be harder than exposing or killing them, and always worth more — each one turned is living proof that belonging doesn't need the king, and Korrven feels it like a re-tuned anchor.

**Secrecy discipline.** Keep the rings honest: feasts-and-rescues Found (nearly everyone) know nothing; the quiet corps knows the story is *managed*; only the Dousers and the Table know pieces of the machinery; only Korrven knows it all — and [Vael curates even him](../vael-the-unmade.md#what-the-bond-costs-korrven).
