# The Encore

**Type:** Movement — part secret audience, part living archive, the only people in Luminar who remember
**Based in:** The Flats and the Fade, [Prismere](../../locations/prismere.md), with quiet rooms behind half the lagoon-towns' sail-lofts
**Alignment:** Ally in potential — and courtable by the wrong collector
**See also:** [Luminar](../kingdoms/luminar.md) · [Religion — Ora](../religion.md#ora-the-first-spark-luminar--creativity) · [Vess Tallow](../../characters/npcs/vess-tallow.md) · [Marlow Pitch](../../characters/npcs/marlow-pitch.md)

## What they are

In a kingdom where only the new can be seen, the deviants are the people who look twice. The Encore is the loose fellowship of those who *keep*: fishers who sing their grandmothers' work-songs unchanged, printers who save one copy of every night's bill, widows who dust drained masterpieces in the Fade, playgoers who walk out of a debut and commit it to memory before morning takes it. Their sign is a single word chalked small near a work worth keeping — **"Again."** — and their gesture is the held clap: hands together, not yet parted, the applause that refuses to end.

Asking for a thing twice is faintly indecent in Luminar — wanting *again* instead of *new*. The Encore does it anyway, behind closed doors, and has for three generations.

## What they want

**Stated:** to keep what was made. To remember the makers. To hear the good ones *again*.

**Real (and mostly unexamined):** they are the audience's half of [Ora's real worship](../religion.md#ora-the-first-spark-luminar--creativity) reviving — the forgotten truth that the spark is a gift *in transit*, which means someone must receive it, and receiving is a devotion. The blank collaborator's corner on every Luminari work is theirs to fill. None of them would put it that way. They just know that when they recite a dead maker's poem in a sail-loft, something in the room feels *fed*.

## How they operate

- **The Recitals** — the masterstroke: kept works performed *again*, from memory, in lofts and net-rooms — the only repertory in Luminar. One company has staged the same beloved play one week a year for thirty years. Audiences leave very quiet, having felt something the Season never gives them: recognition.
- **The Roll of Makers** — names of dead artists and what they made, recited aloud at every gathering, oldest first. The counter-liturgy to the Season's amnesia — and the seed of the canon Luminar has never had.
- **The keeping** — the Fade's caretakers are almost all Encore: they keep the drained works dry, lit, and *visited*. Whether the visiting actually slows the fading, or Encores simply look harder than other people, is yours to decide — the elders swear kept works go stale slower, and they are not wrong often.
- **Pell's book** — the Flats kid who holds all three years of [the Undertide](../../locations/prismere.md#key-features) verbatim is theirs, minded and hidden in plain sight. The Encore understood before anyone that the serial is the first shared story in two hundred years, and they are keeping it the only place it exists: in a person.

## Key Members

- **Tidemistress Halla Gren** — the grey head: chief of the sea-gates, keeper of the Flats' songs, and the one who decides which rooms the Recitals use. Practical to the bone; joined the night her drowned husband's glasswork went stale in her hands.
- **Pell** — the memory: fifteen, fearless, unaware of being the most valuable witness in the kingdom
- **[Vess Tallow](../../characters/npcs/vess-tallow.md)** — not a member, and their living proof: the Encore found her filled corners in the Fade years before the Bill noticed her, and they guard her work like a relic
- **Dree Fanshaw** — the leak-in-reverse: a printer's devil on the Bill who saves one of every playbill and has begun to notice which studios' names stop appearing — the paper trail of [the Northbound](../kingdoms/luminar.md#gm-notes)

## Relationship to the Party

The party's memory in Luminar — the faction that can tell them what stood in an empty studio, who made the drained marvel in the Fade, and what the Undertide actually said in year one. Quiet, unarmed, and priceless: every lever of the arc (the finale war, the Northbound ledger, the re-tuning itself) runs through what the Encore has kept. Their weakness is the mirror of their gift — they are keepers, not fighters, and they have never once acted in public.

## GM Notes

**The name is the battleground.** An encore is a gift called back — or a refusal to let go. The Encore can curdle: keeping becomes hoarding, memory becomes reaction, *again* becomes *never anything new* — a mirror-image of the Season's disease rather than its cure. [Marlow Pitch](../../characters/npcs/marlow-pitch.md) knows exactly which chord to strike: the king's collection preserves *forever* — climate-kept galleries in Commonhold, every kept work safe and seen. An Encore elder who accepts the museum is not a traitor; she is a tired keeper offered a fireproof vault. Track the drift the way the Closed Hand tracks its chalk: does "Again" start appearing on demands instead of masterpieces?

**At the Finale,** the Encore is the answer to the question nobody has asked: *who fills the corner?* The counter-finale ([Prismere — Staging the Finale](../../locations/prismere.md#gm-notes)) needs an audience that can *receive* a completion — and three generations of practiced receiving walk in with the held clap. When the ending lands and the applause comes, the Encore's part is to not let it end: the first public Recital, the Undertide told back to its teller, the city discovering that a finished thing can be kept alive by the keeping.

**Re-tuning role.** Luminar's healing needs Sopharan purpose at the Heart ([anchors file](../anchors-and-engines.md#luminar--the-heart-of-a-thousand-doors)) — but like Sophara's, it needs native graft-stock, and the Encore is it: the living habit of the gift received. Mechanically: the ritual wants the finale given on the Glasshouse stage, a purpose-bearer at the Heart below, and the Encore holding the house — the audience, for the first time in 214 years, answering.
