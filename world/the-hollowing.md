# The Hollowing

**Nature:** The rising of the wilds — the campaign's ambient threat, and the king's engine of consent
**Public knowledge:** Everyone knows the roads are dying. No one knows why.
**See also:** [Geography — Dangerous Wilds](geography.md#dangerous-wilds) · [The Anchors & the Hearts](anchors-and-engines.md) · [Vael, the Unmade](vael-the-unmade.md) · [Korrven](../characters/npcs/the-king.md)

## What everyone knows

The wilds have been getting worse for a generation — grandfathers say the Girdle was safer in their day, and for once the grandfathers are right. Five years ago it stopped being gradual. Caravans that ran for decades now wait for escort or don't run; waystations stand dark; the Girdle's border-forts, enforcing a closure nobody could source, suddenly have all the purpose they can handle. Herders bring the flocks in early. Villages that lost nobody in living memory have started losing people.

Folk call it **the Hollowing** — *the roads are hollowing out* — and nobody remarks that the word rhymes with the name of the March and with the thing in their children's stories ([the Hollow One](religion.md#vael-in-folk-memory--the-hollow-one)). Every kingdom's answer has been the same: higher walls, shorter journeys, fewer reasons to leave. Nobody has noticed that the answer is the problem wearing a helmet.

## Where they come from

All the evidence points inward. The attacks are worst nearest the center of the world; the things retreat toward the March when hunted; and the dead spoke-roads — dead for two centuries — have prints on them again, all headed *out*. For two hundred years the Hollow March kept its horrors to itself, close and quiet, and the border wilds held only what had drifted loose long ago — the voice-things of the Fens, the lights, the shapes at the edge of the Hush. Now the March is **breathing out**, and the wilds are filling.

## The march-things

No two are alike, and all of them are alike in one way: **they wear absence.** The learned of Veritas call them *Hollowborn*; everyone else says *march-things*. They imitate what's missing — a voice that answers in the fog with words you last heard from someone gone; a lit window, warm and familiar, where no house stands; a figure on the road ahead who walks exactly like someone you miss. Killing one leaves no corpse: a pocket of cold quiet, and for a heartbeat everyone nearby feels inexplicably homesick.

Examples, by depth *(sketches — stat and rename freely)*:

- **Strays** *(Tier 1, rim wilds)* — things shaped like lost dogs, strayed lambs, a dropped lantern still burning. They follow lone travelers, patiently, for miles first.
- **The Chorus** *(Tier 1–2, the Fens)* — the voice-packs of the marshes, now hunting in numbers the fen-folk have no songs for.
- **Doorlights** *(Tier 2)* — home, glowing warm, exactly where home isn't.
- **The Missing Man** *(Tier 2–3)* — it has your dead friend's walk. It never has their face. It counts on you needing to check.
- **The Crowd** *(Tier 3–4, the March's edge)* — a mob that exists only when you are alone; its numbers are the inverse of your company.

**The one rule every survivor learns:** the things take the *cut-off*. The straggler, the feuding village, the watchpost that stopped writing home. Company is armor — and it is measured in something realer than headcount: a bickering caravan of twenty is prey; three friends singing are not. People know this the way people know things they can't say aloud without sounding mad.

## The King's Wardens

Into the fear have come the grey cloaks: **the King's Wardens**, companies out of Agoran, riding to any settlement's plea — any kingdom's, no fee, no questions. Their motto is stitched on every cloak: ***No one stands alone.*** They are the finest fighters of march-things alive; they arrive fast, hold walls that would have broken, and leave grain behind them. In a hundred villages across four kingdoms, the fallen Kingdom of Community is the only neighbor who ever came — and the offer that follows the rescue is always gentle: a garrison, a beacon-line, a warden-post on the road. Protection, freely given.

The protection is real. So is what it costs. Every warden-post is the king's eyes, every garrisoned road binds a little tighter to Agoran, and every walled, watched, *grateful* town turns further inward behind its new defenses — which is the one thing the walls cannot keep out.

## GM Notes

**The true cause.** The March is the lattice's drain: for sixty years the Hearts have bled five kingdoms' aspect-stuff down the inverted working, through the keystone's absence, to Vael — and not all of it goes down smooth. Two centuries of unmaking plus sixty years of runoff have *steeped* the March. Since 209, Korrven's re-tuning of Agoran's anchor has strained the lattice — and the same strain that stutters the Mother Engine sloshes the March, and the March breathes out. **The surge is the stutter's sibling**: a second seismograph of the deep machinery, readable by anyone who thinks to chart it.

**What they are.** Vael's lesser kin — not its servants. Vael commands nothing; it *has no force* ([vael-the-unmade.md](vael-the-unmade.md)). The march-things are absence-shaped appetites sharing its one hunger, flowing toward the cut-off the way water moves downhill. Killing them starves nothing. Healing anchors quiets them. Whether anything human is left inside the oldest ones — anything that was caught at the unmaking's edge — decide late; seed never.

**Driven, not commanded.** Korrven cannot speak to them either — nobody can. His quiet corps herds by *cutting*: to aim a tide that flows toward isolation, you carve a channel of it. A beacon doused. A courier who never arrives. A bridge down, a waystation burned — "by monsters." The things follow the cut like water finding the ditch. Survivors of the worst nights sometimes recall that the light on the hill went dark the evening *before*; the few who have compared notes call them **the Dousers**. Discoverables, in escalating order: the sabotage always precedes the attack; the drive-lanes point somewhere; a Douser's route-ledger — beacons and dates, in a warden's hand — matched against attack records is proof.

**The Dousers' second job.** The same crews run **the quiet wagons** — windowless, night-running, on the dead spoke-roads — carrying the black-bagged flagged by [the Gleaner](../characters/npcs/the-gleaner.md) inward to [the Foundation](../locations/the-foundation.md). Renditions travel under the king's seal alone, off every warden ledger Harrow will ever audit. Full pipeline: [the Quiet Harvest](../quests/active/the-quiet-harvest.md).

**The reveal lever.** Equal in tier to the tribunal record: *the shepherd breeds the wolves.* Handle it with the same care — the wardens' heroism is genuine, and most wardens have no idea; the men who fight the things and the men who aim them wear the same grey. Proof lives in three places: the dark ledger, a captured Douser, or **the archives of Veritas** — whose meticulous, hoarded surveys already contain the correlation, unread (strong candidate for the Veritas arc's "hoarded discovery that matters," PLAN Phase 2.5). When it lands, it should land like the tribunal record: kingdoms that took the protection aren't fools freed — they're hostages who now know it.

**Why the fair is never attacked.** The Wayfair's circuit crosses every wild, twice a year, unarmed — and has never lost a wagon. It is the least isolated thing alive; to the march-things it simply doesn't read as prey (the ward-slip helps — [the Wayfair](factions/the-wayfair.md#gm-notes)). Everyone has noticed this without noticing. It cuts both ways: ammunition for a frame-up ("why does the fair cross the Thornfell unscathed?" — feed this straight into the Kaldrum tribunal), and, eventually, the party's insight — *travel the way the fair travels.*

**At the table (Daggerheart).** The Hollowing is the theme with teeth:

- **March-things scale against isolation.** They spotlight the PC with no ally in Close range; give them Fear moves that *separate* — a mimicked voice, a doused light, one PC called away into the dark.
- **Company is armor, mechanically.** A party acting in genuine concert should feel the things hesitate — describe recoil at laughter, at song, at a braided act ([the Braid](magic.md#at-the-daggerheart-table)). A party that fractures gets hunted, and Vael's late-campaign work on the party ([its GM notes](vael-the-unmade.md#gm-notes)) uses the same door.
- **Tier gradient by depth:** rim wilds Tier 1–2; deep wilds Tier 2–3; the March's edge Tier 3–4; the center is Vael's and not a fight.
- **The Fear economy lives here.** When you bank Fear, it's the wilds pressing in; when you spend it, something on the road has noticed the party. Say so out loud sometimes.

**Pressure map — how the tide pushes each kingdom toward him:**

- **Forgeholm:** the Thornfell's watchfires burn a day's ride from the Girdle-gate, and [Krane's](../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md) protection offer grows teeth — wardens against the moor-things, written into the Compact.
- **Sophara:** the Fens causeway — the barge-runs' one exposed leg. [Maro's ambush](../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md#gm-notes), if it comes, is a *driven* attack: the fog answering back on schedule.
- **Veritas:** the Hush is worse and the Screes are emptying eastward; the scholars have charted the whole pattern, beautifully, and told no one.
- **Luminar:** the Fens-lights are taking painters faster than the lagoon-towns will admit, and the coast road now runs escorted.
- **Agoran:** **his roads are the safest in the world.** Five years of surge, and not one Agoran village lost. This is the crack players can find with a map and a question — let them.

**The healing counter.** Every anchor the party re-tunes eases the strain: over the following season, the nearby wilds visibly *quiet* — flocks back out, a waystation relit — living proof the party's method works, and the best propaganda they will ever have. Meanwhile Vael, enraged, drives the tide harder elsewhere ([its counterattack scales](anchors-and-engines.md#gm-notes)): the world gets better where the party has been and worse where they haven't, and the map becomes a scoreboard.

**The Warden-General.** The corps' commander has a face: **[Bram Harrow](../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md)** — the Tier 3 enforcer-lieutenant, running both the grey heroes and the Dousers without ever having laid the two maps side by side. His file holds the ledger, the Harrow's Fold secret, and the three roads out of the reveal.
