# Vael, the Unmade

**Also called:** The Unmade, the Absence, the Old Hunger
**Nature:** Ancient entity — predates the five kingdoms
**Status:** Bound to Korrven; present but not fully manifest

## What It Is

Vael has no origin story anyone living can tell. It is older than the kingdoms, older than the separation, possibly older than the concept of belonging itself. The scholars of Veritas have found references to it in texts so old the language is unrecognizable — always the same shape: a vast, patient emptiness that is drawn to isolation the way a wound is drawn to infection.

It is not evil in any way that can be argued with. It does not want conquest or suffering. It wants *separation*. A world of perfectly isolated things, forever apart, is its paradise. Every closed border, every exile, every kingdom that turns inward feeds it.

It has no name it gave itself, because naming requires community. *Vael* is what the oldest records call it — a word from a dead language that roughly means *the space where something was*. The Unmade is what those who have encountered it and survived tend to call it afterward.

## Vael and the Sundering

Vael could not break the Anchor Working by force — it has no force. Instead it found one of the Working's own designers, a person carrying a private and perfect loneliness, and whispered a single "correction" to the keystone's design. The lattice inverted; Amaranth was unmade; the wards meant to keep Vael out became the walls that feed it. (See [History](history.md) and [The Anchors & the Hearts](anchors-and-engines.md).)

Korrven is not Vael's first partner. He is its second. What became of the first — **the Architect** — is an open question, and possibly a campaign-defining one.

## How It Found Korrven

It didn't find him. He was the most resonant wound it had ever felt from a distance, and it simply moved toward him the way water moves downhill.

When Korrven was exiled — unjustly, furiously, completely — the grief and rage of that moment rang through Vael like a struck bell. Here was a person whose isolation was total, whose wound was real, and whose will was iron. Not a vessel to possess. A *partner*. Someone whose goals and its nature aligned perfectly.

Whether Vael offered itself to Korrven or Korrven sought it out is a matter of interpretation. The result was the same.

## What the Bond Gives Korrven

- His uncanny perception of what people lack — Vael has felt the hunger of isolation in millions of people across centuries. Through it, Korrven can read a kingdom's wound like a page.
- His strength feeding on separation — when kingdoms draw apart, when communities fracture, Vael swells, and Korrven swells with it.
- Something close to unkillability in the conventional sense — Vael cannot be killed, and while the bond holds, neither can Korrven be permanently destroyed by force alone.

## What the Bond Costs Korrven

**The truth.** Vael does not lie to him — it *curates*. It has never told him what his father actually did to the Mother Engine, and never told him whose blood he carries ([the full story](../characters/npcs/the-king.md#gm-notes)) — because the grievance as Korrven tells it, a visionary destroyed for loving the work, is better fuel than the truth. The bond's price is that everything Korrven knows about himself arrived pre-shaped by something that feeds on his isolation. Proof of that concealment is one of the party's few levers against the bond.

**Still open (deliberately):** is there a version of Korrven that could be saved if Vael were severed from him? This is the campaign's final-choice question — *kill the king* vs. *free the man* — and confronting him with the curated truth (the sealed tribunal, his father's real work) is the scene that decides it. See [Korrven — GM Notes](../characters/npcs/the-king.md#gm-notes), "The truth as a weapon."

## How It Can Be Defeated

Vael cannot be killed — it is a concept as much as a being. But it can be *starved*. A world where the five kingdoms are genuinely united, where isolation is healed rather than exploited, leaves Vael with nothing to feed on. It does not die. It diminishes. It waits.

Severing the bond between Vael and Korrven is a different problem — and perhaps a more urgent one. It may be the only way to actually defeat Korrven, and it may also be an act of mercy.

## GM Notes

**The horror of Vael** is that it is not wrong about anything. Separation does exist. Loneliness is real. It didn't create those things — it just found them and called them home.

**Why the bell rang so loud:** Korrven's exile happened in Forgeholm — directly above the Mother Engine, which his father had just secretly tied into the whole lattice. The wound transmitted kingdom-wide because Herrek had built the wire — and it rang in a voice the lattice *knew*, because the family is the Architect's line. Vael has never told Korrven what his father actually did ([the full story](../characters/npcs/the-king.md)) — proof of that concealment is one of the party's few levers for severing the bond.

**First and second partner, one bloodline:** the Architect is Korrven's ancestor ([History — GM Notes](history.md#gm-notes)). The "water moving downhill" above is true as far as it goes — but the channel was dug by Vael's own first partner, and Vael has tracked the line for two centuries. Its second partnership isn't discovery; it's *return*. One more thing it has never told him; one more lever.

**The question it raises:** If Vael is starved by connection, what happens to all the genuine loneliness it was feeding on? Does healing the kingdoms mean those people — Korrven's exiles, his misfits — have somewhere to go? Or do they just become Vael's next meal?

**Vael and the party:** It will perceive the heroes as a threat long before Korrven does. It may try to work on them subtly — isolating them from each other, feeding on whatever fractures exist between them. It does not manipulate through lies. It manipulates through distance.
