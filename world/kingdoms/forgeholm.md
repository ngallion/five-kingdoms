# Forgeholm — Kingdom of Technology

**Aspect:** Technology
**Status:** Isolated, increasingly dangerous — **targeted by the king**

## Character

Forgeholm builds things that work. Its engineers have created wonders of infrastructure — machines that do the labor of a hundred men, weapons of terrifying precision, systems that run without fail. But without philosophy, no one asks whether they *should* build a thing. Without science, they don't fully understand what they've built. Without community, no one asks who it serves.

## What Forgeholm looks like now

Efficient and cold. Everything functions; little is humane. The gap between those who operate the machines and those who are operated *by* them grows wider every year. The technology is not evil, but it is directionless — serving whoever holds power, magnifying whatever will they point it at.

**This is why the king wants it.** Forgeholm's power without Forgeholm's wisdom is exactly what a tyrant needs.

## What they need

Philosophy — to ask whether they should. Science — to understand what they've built. Community — to remember who they're building for.

## GM Notes

> *(Forgeholm is the king's next target. What does the power structure there look like? Is there a faction resisting? How can the party intervene before it falls?)*

**Why the king really wants Forgeholm:** not the weapons — the **Enginewrights' Guild**, the only living holders of the craft that builds and tunes the Hearts. With them, Korrven can re-tune anchor after anchor toward himself. The **Mother Engine** beneath the capital is the master tap on the whole lattice. See [The Anchors & the Hearts](../anchors-and-engines.md).

**The opening hook:** the campaign opens with **[the Wayfair](../factions/the-wayfair.md)** camped outside the forge-city for the first time in living memory — and the Mother Engine stuttering while the fair sings. The Guild wants a culprit; the king's emissary offers them one.

**It's also personal — Forgeholm is his homecoming.** Korrven was exiled from this city in 195 AS, cast out with the rest of Wright Herrek's line ([History — GM Notes](../history.md#gm-notes)).

**The sealed tribunal (195 AS):** in the Guild's deep archive sits the record of Wright Herrek — condemned tamperer of the Mother Engine, exiled with his entire line. The son's name in the ledger: **Korrven**. Guild masters treat the tribunal as shameful precedent, not history; almost no one living has read it. It is the campaign's single best reveal document — proof of who the king is, what his father tried to do, and (to a trained enginewright's eye, e.g. Ondrel Vasht of the Wayfair) proof that the "failed" splice *took*. When Korrven takes the city, or the party beats him to the archive, this comes into play. Full truth: [Korrven](../../characters/npcs/the-king.md).
