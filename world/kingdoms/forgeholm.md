# Forgeholm — Kingdom of Technology

**Aspect:** Technology
**God:** [Ferrun, the Shaping Hand](../religion.md#ferrun-the-shaping-hand-forgeholm--technology) — worshipped now as the Exacting; the Mother Engine occupies his old sanctum
**Status:** Isolated, increasingly dangerous — **targeted by the king**

## Character

Forgeholm builds things that work. Its engineers have created wonders of infrastructure — machines that do the labor of a hundred men, weapons of terrifying precision, systems that run without fail. But without philosophy, no one asks whether they *should* build a thing. Without science, they don't fully understand what they've built. Without community, no one asks who it serves.

## What Forgeholm looks like now

Efficient and cold. Everything functions; little is humane. The gap between those who operate the machines and those who are operated *by* them grows wider every year. The technology is not evil, but it is directionless — serving whoever holds power, magnifying whatever will they point it at.

**This is why the king wants it.** Forgeholm's power without Forgeholm's wisdom is exactly what a tyrant needs.

## What they need

Philosophy — to ask whether they should. Science — to understand what they've built. Community — to remember who they're building for.

## How Forgeholm is ruled

Forgeholm has no crown. The **Enginewrights' Guild** absorbed governance generations ago, and since the Engine Age the absorption is total: Guild law *is* civil law, and **certification is citizenship**. Your marks say what you may operate, repair, own, and pass to your children; the **Unmarked** — the operators, haulers, and sorters who run machines they're forbidden to understand — are legally hands without names. The **Bench of Masters** (twelve sitting masters, chaired by the **First Wright**) certifies, adjudicates, and rules from the Guildhall in [Kaldrum](../../locations/kaldrum.md). Two of its instruments matter most: **line-scouring** (a condemned wright's whole family loses their marks — the law that exiled Korrven), and **quiet retirement** (what happens to senior wrights who ask what the Hearts burn).

## GM Notes

**Role in the arc:** the Tier 1 opening act. The party's job here is to discover the threat, keep the Guild out of the king's hands — or fail instructively — and leave holding the campaign's first real levers: the re-tuning mechanic ([witnessed at the fair](../factions/the-wayfair.md#gm-notes)), the tribunal record, and allies in the Stamp and (possibly) Vorn.

**How the king takes a kingdom that can't be conquered:** not with the army in the Thornfell — that's pressure, not the plan. The plan is **[the Agoran Compact](../../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md#secrets)**: a colossal standing order that gives the forges *direction* (his standing offer to Forgeholm, [poison included](../../characters/npcs/the-king.md#he-knows-the-wound)) while moving certification, clause by clause, under his provisioning authority — inspectors at the Root by season's end. The Mother Engine's stutter hands Krane her second act: a culprit (the fair) and a protection offer. Forgeholm is meant to *invite* him in.

**The resistance:** **[the Second Stamp](../factions/the-second-stamp.md)** — the illegal repair-fellowship of the Chimneys, pre-political at campaign start, politicized in real time by the Compact and the frame-up. It is also splittable: Korrven's marks-for-the-Unmarked policy is half of what these people ever wanted. Which way the Stamp breaks is the arc's barometer.

**How the party intervenes** — three levers, any of which can carry the arc:
1. **The tribunal defense.** When the Guild moves against the Wayfair, someone has to argue where nobody argued in 195 — same hall, same anvil ([the centerpiece scene](../../characters/npcs/halda-vorn.md#gm-notes)).
2. **The measurement.** Vorn follows numbers anywhere. Proving the stutter's real cause (the lattice under strain, not sabotage) — likely with Ondrel Vasht's help — flips the Guild's ruler and collapses the frame-up ([her arc](../../characters/npcs/halda-vorn.md#gm-notes)).
3. **The archive race.** The sealed tribunal record proves who the king is before he arrives. Krane has standing orders to take it; getting there first turns the campaign's best reveal document into the party's ([staging](../../locations/kaldrum.md#gm-notes)).

**What healing looks like:** re-tune the Mother Engine with what Forgeholm lacks — **Sophara's question** (*should we?*) and **Agoran's answer** (*for whom?*), physically present at the Heart per [the re-tuning rules](../anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine), grafted onto the Stamp's living habit of the named repair ([their role in the ritual](../factions/the-second-stamp.md#gm-notes)). Healed, Forgeholm doesn't slow down — it starts *consecrating* again: works with their purpose spoken aloud, [Ferrun's real worship](../religion.md#ferrun-the-shaping-hand-forgeholm--technology) returned. One caution flag: the Mother Engine is the **master tap** — destroying *this* anchor, unlike any other, touches the whole lattice. Leave the consequences deliberately undefined until a player reaches for the lever.

**If the party fails,** Kaldrum falls by invitation and the homecoming happens ([contingency](../../locations/kaldrum.md#gm-notes)) — the campaign continues as liberation rather than defense. Losing here should hurt, not end the game.

**Why the king really wants Forgeholm:** not the weapons — the **Enginewrights' Guild**, the only living holders of the craft that builds and tunes the Hearts. With them, Korrven can re-tune anchor after anchor toward himself. The **Mother Engine** beneath the capital is the master tap on the whole lattice. See [The Anchors & the Hearts](../anchors-and-engines.md).

**The opening hook:** the campaign opens with **[the Wayfair](../factions/the-wayfair.md)**'s solstice fair camped outside the forge-city — a familiar, welcome fixture on its regular circuit — and the Mother Engine stuttering city-wide while the fair sings, something no fair has ever done before. The Guild wants a culprit; the king's emissary offers them one.

**It's also personal — Forgeholm is his homecoming.** Korrven was exiled from this city in 195 AS, cast out with the rest of Wright Herrek's line ([History — GM Notes](../history.md#gm-notes)).

**The sealed tribunal (195 AS):** in the Guild's deep archive sits the record of Wright Herrek — condemned tamperer of the Mother Engine, exiled with his entire line. The son's name in the ledger: **Korrven**. Guild masters treat the tribunal as shameful precedent, not history; almost no one living has read it. It is the campaign's single best reveal document — proof of who the king is, what his father tried to do, and (to a trained enginewright's eye, e.g. Ondrel Vasht of the Wayfair) proof that the "failed" splice *took*. When Korrven takes the city, or the party beats him to the archive, this comes into play. Sealed alongside the record are Herrek's confiscated effects — including a sheaf of pre-Sundering schematics no Guild master has ever understood: **the Architect's notes**, the family inheritance that guided both the anchor-site discovery of 152 AS and Herrek's splice ([History — GM Notes](../history.md#gm-notes)). Full truth: [Korrven](../../characters/npcs/the-king.md).
