# Agoran — Kingdom of Community

**Aspect:** Community
**God:** [Tavan, the Long Table](../religion.md#tavan-the-long-table-agoran--community) — his liturgy now occupied by Korrven; grace is said in the king's name
**Status:** **Fallen — under the king's control**

## Character

Agoran was once the warmest place in the world. People came from everywhere to feel a sense of belonging, to find their people, to be part of something larger than themselves. In isolation, that warmth became hollow. No creativity meant no new traditions, no songs, no art. No philosophy meant no shared values beyond proximity. No science meant no understanding of the wider world. Community became an end in itself — togetherness for its own sake, with nothing to hold it together.

And into that hollow, the king walked.

## What Agoran looks like now

On the surface: orderly, united, purposeful. The king has given Agoran what it craved — direction, identity, an enemy to define itself against. The people of Agoran are genuinely grateful to him. That is the horror of it. The community is real. The belonging is real. The cost is invisible to those inside it.

Beneath the surface: dissenters who remember what Agoran was, exiles who fled and carry the old culture, and the king's enforcers who ensure the story stays clean.

## What Agoran needs

To be reminded that belonging built on exclusion and fear is not community. Creativity to give it songs and traditions again. Philosophy to give it values. This is the hardest kingdom to "heal" — the wound is that it feels healed.

## How Agoran is ruled

Agoran has a king and no court. Korrven governs from the head of the long tables at [Commonhold](../../locations/commonhold.md)'s moot-ring — no palace, no throne, no decree that does not arrive sounding like an invitation. The old institutions were not abolished; they were *filled*: the harvest moot is now his audience, the ring-barns fly his banners, the grace is said in his name in [Tavan's own cadences](../religion.md#tavan-the-long-table-agoran--community).

The working parts:

- **The King's Table** — his inner circle, seated (literally) at the head table: [Warden-General Bram Harrow](../../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md) (the wardens and the roads), [First Steward Iveth Kall](../../characters/npcs/iveth-kall.md) (the rolls, the feasts, the granaries), and abroad, [Ilsabet Krane](../../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md) (provisioning and the Forgeholm Compact). Behind them all, [the Found](../factions/the-found.md) — the movement of the once-lost that is Agoran's administrative spine.
- **The rolls of belonging** — every Agoran entered at a table, fed, named, visited, *known*. The census is care, and total. See [the Hall of Names](../../locations/commonhold.md#the-shape-of-the-city).
- **The wardens** — the grey cloaks: border companies facing outward, the [King's Wardens](../the-hollowing.md#the-kings-wardens) riding abroad as heroes, and the quiet corps within them that keeps the king's story clean.
- **The Setting-Out** — the kingdom's only punishment: struck from every roll, fed like the guest, never again inside. In the age of [the Hollowing](../the-hollowing.md), being unseated is a death sentence no one has to carry out. See [Commonhold — Key Features](../../locations/commonhold.md#key-features).

## GM Notes

**Role in the arc:** the Tier 3 strike and the campaign's climax — but Agoran should be *felt* from Session 1: the grain-carts at Kaldrum's gates, the grey cloaks riding to the rescue, the refugees on the fork island, the exiles' songs. The party will meet fallen Agoran's kindness long before they see its capital.

**The problem stated plainly:** liberation is impossible by force, because the people are not oppressed — they are grateful, fed, safe, and *right* to feel all three. Any army the party raises against Agoran proves the king's story (an enemy at last, exactly as promised). The campaign must win Agorans, not defeat them; Korrven's power fails only when his own kingdom stops flowing through him ([the anchor](../anchors-and-engines.md#agoran--the-warm-heart-now-the-kings-heart)).

**Three levers — all of them needed, none sufficient alone:**

1. **The herding proof.** *The shepherd breeds the wolves* ([The Hollowing — GM Notes](../the-hollowing.md#gm-notes)): the Dousers' dark ledger, the drive-lanes, the safest-roads crack seeded below. It indicts him to every kingdom at once — and, harder and better, to his own wardens. [Harrow](../../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md#gm-notes) is the door: the corps follows him, and he has never let himself draw the map.
2. **The old grace.** Memory as insurgency: [the Eleventh Chair](../factions/the-eleventh-chair.md) has kept the songs, graces, and naming-days the Hearts and the king between them hollowed out. Every verse the party helps carry home is re-tuning material — Agoran's *real* aspect, warmth toward the stranger, set against belonging-with-an-enemy. The centerpiece is [Oren Brandt's return to the Ring](../../characters/npcs/oren-brandt.md#gm-notes), and [Iveth Kall](../../characters/npcs/iveth-kall.md#gm-notes) deciding which grace the city sings.
3. **The truth about the king.** The sealed tribunal record and the curated bond ([Korrven — GM Notes](../../characters/npcs/the-king.md#gm-notes)): vindication and indictment on one page, and proof that Vael feeds him his own story pre-shaped. This is the lever on the man rather than the kingdom — the *kill the king vs. free the man* material — and it lands hardest at his own table, in front of the people he saved.

**What healing looks like:** re-tune the Warm Heart with what Agoran lacks — Luminar's creativity (new songs at the long tables, the first new tradition in living memory) and Sophara's philosophy (values beyond proximity; a belonging that can survive disagreement). Healed, Agoran doesn't cool — it opens: the guest's chair means *the guest* again, the door held open instead of held shut, and the warmth flowing outward to strangers instead of upward to a king. Per [the anchors file](../anchors-and-engines.md#agoran--the-warm-heart-now-the-kings-heart), this anchor "needs everything" — stage it as the one re-tuning that requires bearers of *all* the other kingdoms at the Hearth at once: the campaign's whole method, performed in front of the man it defeats.

**If the party comes early:** let them. The door is open — that is the trap ([Commonhold — GM Notes](../../locations/commonhold.md#gm-notes)). An early Agoran visit is espionage under perfect hospitality: everything easy, everything watched, every kindness billed later. It is also the best place in the world to learn what they're actually fighting — nobody leaves Commonhold still believing the enemy is an army.

**The safest roads in the world.** Five years of [the Hollowing](../the-hollowing.md), and not one Agoran village lost — his herders aim the tide *away* from home, and ordinary Agorans read their safety as proof the king protects his own. It is the discoverable crack: any player who lays the attack reports against a map will see the hole in the pattern, shaped exactly like Agoran. The Eleventh Chair's old drovers noticed it first ([their file](../factions/the-eleventh-chair.md#gm-notes)); nobody has believed them yet.
