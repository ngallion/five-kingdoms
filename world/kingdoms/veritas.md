# Veritas — Kingdom of Science

**Aspect:** Science
**Status:** Isolated, deeply insular

## Character

Veritas is the most rigorous place in the world. Its scholars have mapped the stars, catalogued every living thing, and unlocked principles of the natural world that other kingdoms can barely comprehend. But without community, their discoveries are never shared. Without creativity, their methods have become rigid orthodoxy. Without philosophy, they cannot agree on what knowledge is *for*.

## What Veritas looks like now

A kingdom of brilliant individuals who barely talk to each other. Grand libraries full of work no one reads. Academies where scholars publish into silence. The people of Veritas are suspicious of outsiders (and of each other) — knowledge is hoarded, not shared. Progress has slowed to a crawl, not from lack of intelligence, but from lack of collaboration.

The deepest vaults hold the oldest holdings in the world: pre-Sundering texts in a language no living scholar reads, rotting trade ledgers, records catalogued centuries ago and never opened since. Veritas hoards even from itself.

## What they need

Community — to share discovery, to build on each other's work. Creativity — to imagine beyond what can currently be measured.

## GM Notes

> *(What role does Veritas play in the campaign arc? What would it look like for Veritas to heal?)*

**The archives are the setting's buried library.** Established across the lore files — Veritas holds, unread and unreadable, the corpus that could explain everything:

- **The Vael references** — texts so old the language is unrecognizable, always describing the same shape: a vast, patient emptiness drawn to isolation ([Vael, the Unmade](../vael-the-unmade.md)). This is the only place in the world where Vael is *written down*.
- **The pre-Sundering trade ledgers** listing tariffs for **six** trade partners — the sixth name blurs on the page and slides out of memory minutes after reading ([History — What Historians Got Wrong](../history.md#what-historians-got-wrong)).
- More broadly, the pre-Sundering texts that could unravel the whole official history ([Anchors & Engines — Who Knows What](../anchors-and-engines.md#who-knows-what-gm-reference)) — hoarded, in a dead language, catalogued and never read.

The bitter joke: the kingdom of Science possesses the answer to the world's central mystery and is structurally incapable of reading it — translation would require *collaboration*, the exact thing the Silent Heart drinks ([Anchors & Engines](../anchors-and-engines.md#veritas--the-silent-heart)). Getting something out of these vaults is the natural spine of the Veritas arc, and the "scholar who knows too much and tells no one" (PLAN Phase 2.5, NPC still to create) is its natural gatekeeper.
