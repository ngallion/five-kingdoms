# Veritas — Kingdom of Science

**Aspect:** Science
**God:** [Theora, the Clear Eye](../religion.md#theora-the-clear-eye-veritas--science) — worshipped now as the Silent Witness, shown everything and told nothing
**Status:** Isolated, deeply insular — **and the king's Correspondent is the first audience its scholars have had in living memory**

## Character

Veritas is the most rigorous place in the world. Its scholars have mapped the stars, catalogued every living thing, and unlocked principles of the natural world that other kingdoms can barely comprehend. But without community, their discoveries are never shared. Without creativity, their methods have become rigid orthodoxy. Without philosophy, they cannot agree on what knowledge is *for*.

## What Veritas looks like now

A kingdom of brilliant individuals who barely talk to each other. Grand libraries full of work no one reads. Academies where scholars publish into silence. The people of Veritas are suspicious of outsiders (and of each other) — knowledge is hoarded, not shared. Progress has slowed to a crawl, not from lack of intelligence, but from lack of collaboration.

The mechanism under the silence *(GM)*: [the Silent Heart](../anchors-and-engines.md#veritas--the-silent-heart) drinks **shared discovery** — knowledge spoken aloud is knowledge spent. Every Veritan scholar has felt an insight dim in the telling of it; none of them has ever said so out loud, which is the wound eating its own evidence. The hoarding is not temperament. It is two centuries of correct, unexamined flinch.

The deepest vaults hold the oldest holdings in the world: pre-Sundering texts catalogued centuries ago and never opened since. The strange part — noted in the catalogues, pursued by no one — is *which* texts went dark. Every kingdom reads its own two-century-old records without trouble; it is the era's **diplomatic corpus** — treaties, trade ledgers, letters between crowns, all in the shared script scholars call the **Concord Tongue** — that no living person can read. Veritas hoards even from itself.

## What they need

Community — to share discovery, to build on each other's work. Creativity — to imagine beyond what can currently be measured.

## How Veritas is ruled

Sovereignty belongs to **the Assay** — the tribunal of nine senior scholars that verifies claims of discovery and assigns **priority**: the formal ruling of who knew a thing *first*. Priority is Veritas's only currency. Standing, instruments, vault-allotments, a tower of one's own — all of it flows from verified firsts, and none of it requires that anyone actually *read* the work. A scholar seals a discovery in the **Claim-Vault**, dated and witnessed shut; disputes are settled by comparing seals, never contents. Scholarship as land-claim: the kingdom of knowing runs on dates, not knowledge.

The Assay's presiding voice is **the Arbiter of Record**, keeper of the Great Catalogue — the index of everything Veritas has ever claimed to know. The seat has been held for twenty-six years by **[Vesna Corle](../../characters/npcs/vesna-corle.md)**, whose exactness is the kingdom's exactness. The Assay meets in the one chamber in [Meridian](../../locations/meridian.md) where scholars are *required* to speak, and has evolved procedure to speak as little as possible: claims presented as numbers, findings cited by sealed page, verdicts by show of hands.

What actually keeps the kingdom alive is everything the Catalogue doesn't rank: the **Stacks-crews** — the copyists, binders, and porters who handle every book in the Great Archive and are the only people who know where anything *is* — the instrument-makers of the Verniers, and the irrigated farm towns of the plateau's fringe, who feed ten thousand scholars and appear in the records chiefly as a column of grain-weights.

One more Veritan peculiarity, load-bearing *(GM)*: **data flows freely; meaning is hoarded.** The heliograph towers flash raw measurements between observatories all day — numbers, positions, counts, shared without a flicker of reluctance. It is *conclusions* that are never spoken. Veritans learned, without ever saying it, that a table of figures costs nothing to give away and an insight costs something real ([the Heart drinks the transfer of understanding, not ink](../anchors-and-engines.md#veritas--the-silent-heart)). The whole kingdom is a body that circulates blood and refuses to think.

## The priority wars

The kingdom file's coldest truth — knowledge hoarded, not shared — looks like this at the table:

- **The graveyard of duplicates.** The Claim-Vault's lower galleries hold the same discoveries proved over and over: three towers spending thirty years each on one theorem, none knowing of the others, all sealed within a decade. The kingdom's genius is spent re-deriving itself. Nobody audits this; the audit would require reading.
- **Claim-jumping.** Date-forgery is Veritas's one native crime of passion — re-inked seals, bribed witnesses, a rival's courier delayed three days in the Hem. The Assay punishes it with **unranking**: every priority struck, the scholar's name removed from the Catalogue. The unranked haunt the Hem and the [Screes](../geography.md#the-screes-forgeholm--veritas) road; some walk to the [Wayfair](../factions/the-wayfair.md) and never come back.
- **The unasked question.** Asking a Veritan what they are working on is obscene — like asking a stranger's bank balance. Conversation here is a lattice of careful nothing: weather, instruments, grain prices. Two scholars can share a stair for twenty years and know each other only by footstep.

**In play:** the wound lands fastest through polite refusal. A PC asks an urgent question — the answer exists, forty people in the room know it — and meets the gentle, immovable formula: *"That is not yet sealed."* Nothing shows the kingdom's sickness faster than watching it choose a date-stamp over a life.

## GM Notes

**The archives are the setting's buried library.** Established across the lore files — Veritas holds, unread and unreadable, the corpus that could explain everything:

- **The Vael references** — texts so old the language is unrecognizable, always describing the same shape: a vast, patient emptiness drawn to isolation ([Vael, the Unmade](../vael-the-unmade.md)). This is the only place in the world where Vael is *written down*. A genuinely ancient corpus, in a genuinely (mundanely) dead language — far older than the Sundering. **[Edda Prine](../../characters/npcs/edda-prine.md) has read it.**
- **The pre-Sundering trade ledgers** listing tariffs for **six** trade partners — the sixth name blurs on the page and slides out of memory minutes after reading ([History — What Historians Got Wrong](../history.md#what-historians-got-wrong)). Written in kingdom scripts, so they *can* be read — the magic only eats the name.
- **The Concord Tongue corpus** — the inter-kingdom texts that could unravel the whole official history ([Anchors & Engines — Who Knows What](../anchors-and-engines.md#who-knows-what-gm-reference)). Not merely unread: **unreadable**, because the Concord Tongue was Amaranth's own language and was unmade with it. Study refuses to stick — a scholar can copy the glyphs and lose the grammar by morning ([History — GM Notes](../history.md#gm-notes)).

The bitter joke: the kingdom of Science possesses the answer to the world's central mystery and is structurally incapable of reading it — translation would require *collaboration*, the exact thing the Silent Heart drinks — and the key texts are locked in a language the Sundering itself keeps dead, so cracking them means collaborating with the *erased*: the [Wayfair](../factions/the-wayfair.md)'s preserved liturgy, or a descendant of Amaranth. See **the Reading**, below.

**Role in the arc:** Tier 2, the rally phase — and the campaign's *intelligence* payoff. [Re-tuning the Silent Heart needs an infusion of fellowship](../anchors-and-engines.md#veritas--the-silent-heart) — Agoran's gift — and Agoran belongs to the king, so the fellowship must come from the people who carried it out: [the Eleventh Chair](../factions/the-eleventh-chair.md). [Oren Brandt](../../characters/npcs/oren-brandt.md) is the natural bearer (the last moot-speaker, teaching the old grace at the world's last real harvest moot), or a fork-island exile, or an Agoran PC. Run after Sophara and the party can arrive with the camp's trust already earned; run earlier and they must win their bearer the hard way. Either way, what the party carries *out* of Veritas — the content of the Reading — arms the endgame: it is the proof of [Vael's curation of Korrven](../vael-the-unmade.md#what-the-bond-costs-korrven) that the sever-the-bond lever runs on.

**The push — three pressures converging:**

1. **The Concordance.** [Ansel Marr](../../characters/npcs/ansel-marr.md)'s great project: the king's universal catalogue, and the first *audience* Veritas has had in two centuries. Send your work north and it is read, answered, used, built upon — the letters home from Commonhold all agree, in oddly similar phrases, that their work finally *means something*. Manuscripts go north by the crate now; scholars follow their manuscripts; towers along the survey lines are going dark. And Marr's private errand rides inside the public one: a want-list, in a hand that is not the king's, hunting the oldest corpus in the Deep Stacks — to be located, crated, and shipped north **sealed, unread** *(see his file — the clause is the seam)*.
2. **The Last Reader is dying.** [Edda Prine](../../characters/npcs/edda-prine.md) is the only person alive who can read the Vael corpus, her understanding is a lifetime deep, and [the Heart drinks the transfer](../anchors-and-engines.md#veritas--the-silent-heart): she has one whole telling in her, and loses what she tells. She has been saving it for the right ear for thirty years while her body runs out. Marr courts her hardest of anyone in the kingdom.
3. **The pattern nobody reports.** The [Hush](../geography.md#the-hush-veritas--sophara) is worse, the [Screes](../geography.md#the-screes-forgeholm--veritas) are emptying eastward, and the fringe towns have started losing flocks, then shepherds ([the Hollowing](../the-hollowing.md)). The survey offices have charted the whole surge — beautifully, precisely, in triplicate — and told no one, each office assuming its chart is the only one. Meanwhile the King's Wardens have begun offering escorts on the Girdle east of the Screes crossing, and the farm towns, unconsulted and unprotected by Meridian, are starting to accept.

**Three levers — any of which can carry the arc:**

1. **The Last Reader.** Win Prine's one telling before the king does — and *build her the right room to give it in*. Her wound is to be understood, once, before the end; that is exactly what the Concordance offers her. The party's counter isn't an argument, it's fellowship: at a [Loosening](../factions/the-wayfair.md#the-loosening) — or at a re-tuned Heart — a Veritan can teach and be *enriched* by it. Bring the fair to her, or her to the fair, and she can give the telling and keep it: the first teacher in Veritas in living memory to end a lecture larger than she began it. Proof, in one old woman, that the party's method works.
2. **The Reading.** Crack the Concord corpus. The pieces exist and have never been in one room: the **glyphs** (the Deep Stacks' sealed galleries), the **sound** (the [Wayfair](../factions/the-wayfair.md)'s liturgy and lullaby — Amara's tongue, preserved as rote song for two centuries by people who never knew what they were carrying), and a **braid of fellowship** to hold what the unmaking eats. Staged at the Heart, the Reading *is* the re-tuning — see [Meridian — Staging the Reading](../../locations/meridian.md#gm-notes).
3. **The Concordance ledger.** Expose what the pipeline costs: the rhyming letters, the darkened towers, the northbound scholars whose priority claims sailed through the Assay suspiciously fast — and the want-list clause no loyal servant of a knowledge-loving king can explain. The crowd to turn is small and specific: **the Assay itself**, and above all [Vesna Corle](../../characters/npcs/vesna-corle.md), who can open the Deep Stacks lawfully — or seal them forever. Show her what the Catalogue is being *used for*, and the kingdom's whole apparatus of verification turns on the king's man in a single sitting.

**What healing looks like:** re-tune the Silent Heart with **Agoran fellowship** present at the first shared discovery in two centuries — the Reading itself, landed in company. Healed, Veritas doesn't stop measuring — it starts *teaching*: the lecture returns as [Theora's sacrament](../religion.md#theora-the-clear-eye-veritas--science), truth passed voice to voice and growing in the passage; the Archive opens reading rooms; the Assay verifies by replication *together* instead of comparing seals; the heliographs start carrying meaning as well as numbers; the Marginalia step out of the margins and hold the first open colloquium since the Sundering; and in the observatory at Zero, after 214 years of the Silent Witness, [Theora says one exact sentence](../religion.md#gm-notes). The kingdom of knowing discovers what it was for: some truths cannot be known alone.

**If the party fails:** no armies. The corpus goes north crated as an honor — the heart of the Concordance Hall in Commonhold, catalogued forever, opened never *(which is the point; see Korrven's stake)*. Prine dies untold, or spends her one telling into Marr's kind, careful ear, and the only reading of Vael ever made passes to the thing it describes. The Assay, its currency quietly debased, certifies what it is sent; the heliographs carry the king's figures; and Veritas ends as the registry of the new world — the kingdom that stamps the official truth of a man becoming a god. It never notices it has fallen. It files the annexation under the year received.

**Korrven's stake — and the seam in it.** A god needs a scripture. His self-apotheosis ([Religion — GM Notes](../religion.md#gm-notes)) needs the record kept right — history certified, priority assigned, the Catalogue's authority behind the story he tells — and Veritas is where truth is stamped. He also genuinely wants the Archive's lattice-lore read and used; knowledge is the one wealth he has never despised. **Which is why the sealed-unread clause on the oldest corpus is not his.** Vael curates: the Deep Stacks are the one place in the world where its two-century concealment — the Architect's hand, the bloodline, what it has never told its partner — is written down, and it has steered Korrven into ordering his own vindication crated shut. The Veritas arc is where the party can first put a visible crack between the king and his god — and if they've done the Reading, they leave holding the crowbar. → [Vael — What the Bond Costs Korrven](../vael-the-unmade.md#what-the-bond-costs-korrven)
