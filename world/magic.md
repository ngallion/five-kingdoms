# Magic — Strandcraft & the Braid

**Nature:** How magic works — what it draws on, what the Sundering did to it, and what the party can do that almost no one living can
**Public knowledge:** Every kingdom knows its own tradition and assumes it is all magic ever was. Almost no one remembers magic was ever shared.
**See also:** [History](history.md) · [The Anchors & the Hearts](anchors-and-engines.md) · [Religion — The Six](religion.md) · [The Wayfair](factions/the-wayfair.md)

## What Magic Is

All magic in this world is **aspect-work**: drawing on one of the six fundamental strands of what humans are — creativity, knowledge, craft, wisdom, community, and the sixth no one remembers. The strands are not metaphors. They are real currents in the world, old enough that the gods themselves are elder concentrations of them ([Religion — GM Notes](religion.md#gm-notes)), and everything called magic — a glamour, a scrying, a blessing, the Anchor Working itself — is a way of channeling a strand through a living will.

Before the Sundering, the practice was called **strandcraft**, and its practitioners **weavers** — the same weaving metaphor the old faith used for itself, because they were never really separate things. A spell was a small act of what the Concord did at scale.

## The Braid

The first thing every apprentice weaver once learned: **one strand alone flickers; two braided hold.** Magic worked in a single aspect was possible but thin — a spark with nothing to catch on. Real workings braided strands: art shaped by purpose, knowledge given to fellowship, craft guided by wisdom. The parallel to the Weave's one commandment — *no god is worshipped alone* — was not a parallel. It was the same law, observed from the other side.

The great workings of the Age of the Weave — the Concord Roads that never wash out, the six-paired solstice rites, and finally the Anchor Working — were braids of all six strands at once. Nothing on that scale has been attempted since the Sundering. Nothing on that scale is currently *possible*. Weavers who try report the same thing in five different vocabularies: the strands will not take hold of each other. Something that used to be between them is gone.

## What the Sundering Did to Magic

Before the Sundering, modest magic was an everyday craft — a hedge-weaver in every village, a blessing over every keel and kiln. Two things ended that world:

**The inverted lattice sorted the strands.** Each anchor, turned inward, concentrates its kingdom's own aspect inside its borders and thins the other five ([Anchors & Engines](anchors-and-engines.md)). Within a generation, each kingdom's weavers found that only one strand still answered reliably — their own — and that it answered *uncannily well*. Five narrow, brilliant, warped traditions grew where one shared craft had been. Cross-strand magic inside a kingdom's borders is now close to impossible for a lone caster; most traditions have forgotten it was ever real, and file the old braided workings under myth.

**The Hearts drink from the same well.** Since the Engine Age, each kingdom's Heart has been extracting its aspect as fuel — and casters, who channel the aspect directly, feel the drain most keenly. Every kingdom's magical tradition has a hole in it shaped exactly like what its Heart eats. The traditions have theologized the hole, the way the cults theologized the Silence. None of them has noticed it is recent.

Magic is therefore *withered*: rarer than it was, narrower than it was, and locally deeper than it ever was. A common person may go years without seeing a working. Every kingdom assumes this is the natural order.

## The Five Traditions

### Luminar — the Brightwork

Glamour, illusion, inspiration, works of light and sound; magic as performance. Luminari weavers can conjure wonders faster than anywhere in the world — and nothing they make *lasts*. Enchantments fray at the moment of completion; the tradition has responded by never completing anything, leaving works deliberately unfinished so the magic holds ([the Heart of a Thousand Doors](anchors-and-engines.md#luminar--the-heart-of-a-thousand-doors) drinks completion). A Brightwork masterpiece is a permanent rough draft, maintained forever.

### Veritas — the Exact Art

Divination, scrying, measurement, the silent glyph-script that holds meaning without speech. Veritan magic sees farther and truer than any other — and cannot be shared. A scrying described aloud dims; a taught glyph weakens in the teacher's hand. Veritan adepts work alone, vault their notations, and believe (correctly, for the wrong reason) that secrecy is a technical requirement of the art ([the Silent Heart](anchors-and-engines.md#veritas--the-silent-heart) drinks the transfer).

### Forgeholm — Runework

Magic bound into made things: runes of holding, edge-litanies, engines that run a little better than their tolerances allow. The Guild certifies runework as a branch of engineering and would be offended to hear it called magic. It is the most reliable tradition in the five kingdoms and the least alive: a runeworked tool does exactly what it does, and no wright can any longer bind a *purpose* into a thing — the old consecration "this plow for the Granary" comes out as dead metal ([the Mother Engine](anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine) drinks the why).

### Sophara — the Considered Art

Wards, abjuration, magic of mind and meaning: workings that hold, weigh, question, and protect. Sopharan wards are the finest in the world and can stand for decades. What the tradition cannot do is *strike* — any working that requires a moment of decisive release fails at the release, and the schools have concluded such magic is philosophically incoherent anyway ([the Still Heart](anchors-and-engines.md#sophara--the-still-heart) drinks the moment between decision and act). A Sopharan battle-mage is a contradiction in terms. This was not always true.

### Agoran — Hearthbinding

Oaths, blessings, circles of protection, magic worked *by many hands at once* — the only tradition that was ever collaborative, because its strand is. Old hearthbinding blessed harvests, sealed guest-rights, and knit villages through winter. Under Korrven it still works — better than in living memory, Agorans say — but every circle now closes toward the King's Table, and an Agoran oath sworn today binds, subtly, *to him* ([the Warm Heart](anchors-and-engines.md#agoran--the-warm-heart-now-the-kings-heart), partially re-tuned). The warmth is real. That is the trap.

## Divine Magic & the Silence

Clerics, seraphs, and the faithful channel their god's aspect directly, and the channel still works — the aspect is real even where the god is silent ([Religion — the Silence](religion.md#the-silence-of-the-gods)). Divine casters are, without knowing it, the least warped weavers alive: they draw from the strand's deep source rather than a kingdom's tradition. They also feel the Silence most personally. Prayer works like a well-maintained pump over a well no one has heard water in for two centuries.

## Magic for Travelers — and the Party

Because the warping is enforced by the lattice, not by blood, **it loosens with distance from the anchors**. In the border wilds between kingdoms, all strands answer a little; on Wayfair fairgrounds during the Loosening, braiding briefly works the way the old books describe ([The Wayfair](factions/the-wayfair.md#the-loosening)).

A party drawn from several kingdoms, traveling together, is therefore something the five traditions say cannot exist: weavers of different strands in one company, in the thin places, learning to braid. Small braided workings — one PC's magic shaped by another's knowledge, art raised over shared labor — are the first stirring of pre-Sundering magic in 214 years. NPC weavers who witness one will not have words for what they saw. Some will follow the party just to see it again.

## At the Daggerheart Table

**Domains and strands — a loose map.** No mechanical changes: any class and domain works for a character from any kingdom. But when players want their magic to *feel* like home, this is how the nine domains most naturally read against the strands:

| Domain | Reads most naturally as | Flavor |
|--------|------------------------|--------|
| **Arcana** | the raw strands, unbraided | the oldest magic — wild, instinctive, pre-tradition |
| **Blade** | Technology | Ferrun's edge-litanies; the weapon as certified work |
| **Bone** | Technology / Community | the body as instrument — forge discipline or shared labor |
| **Codex** | Science | Veritan glyphwork; magic as learned notation |
| **Grace** | Creativity | the Brightwork; performance, presence, the held breath |
| **Midnight** | the dark between strands | secrecy and absence — the Hollow One's weather, not its service |
| **Sage** | Community | the Granary's magic: growth, harvest, the living land |
| **Splendor** | *(no kingdom claims it)* | healing and mending persist everywhere, oddly ownerless — see GM Notes |
| **Valor** | Philosophy | resolve enacted — the verdict, the stand, the settled scale |

A Veritan wizard's Codex spells are silent glyphs; an Agoran troubadour's Grace is table-song; a Sopharan guardian's Valor is the one settled verdict their whole kingdom can't reach. Reskin freely.

**The Braid (light mechanic).** When two or more PCs deliberately combine *different* aspects toward a single goal — a working shaped by one and aimed by another's knowledge, a plan argued in Sopharan terms and executed with Forgeholm hands — lean generous with **Hope**: grant a Hope to the participants, or improve the result a step. Once per scene is plenty. This extends the standing Loosening guidance ([Wayfair — table notes](factions/the-wayfair.md#gm-notes)) from fairgrounds to the party itself: the party *is* a small traveling Loosening. Near a Heart, a braided act should make the extraction visibly stutter — lights dim, the hum skips — which is the party's first breadcrumb toward the re-tuning mechanic.

## GM Notes

**The braid is Amara.** The secret under the whole system: strands do not bind each other. They never did. Every braid — every act of magic that joins two aspects — works *through* the sixth strand, Love, the "and" of the world ([Religion — Amara](religion.md#gm-notes)). This is why braiding nearly died at the Sundering: the glue was unmade with Amaranth. It is why braided magic feels warm, why the Loosening feels the way it does, why the great workings are impossible now, and why the Anchor Working needed Amaranth as keystone — a braid of six could only be tied *by* the sixth. Whenever the party braids, they are drawing on what remains of an unmade god. They are also, in the smallest way, feeding it: every braid is a coin in the well of Amara's return. If the campaign ends with the keystone of Love restored ([the endgame](anchors-and-engines.md#the-endgame--the-keystone-problem)), the party has been casting the resurrection spell since session 1, one Hope at a time.

**Splendor is Amara's strand.** Healing belongs to no kingdom because mending — restoring what belongs together — was Amaranth's magic. Every healer in the five kingdoms is unknowingly working the erased strand; it survived the unmaking because no kingdom's anchor claims it, so no Heart can drink it. A Splendor-domain PC is a walking seed of the sixth-aspect reveal: their magic has no tradition, no temple, and no explanation *anywhere they look* — until the Wayfair's elders go very quiet watching them work.

**Reveal pacing.** The chain runs: braiding works for the party (odd) → braiding works *better* near the Wayfair and in border wilds (pattern) → every tradition's hole matches its Heart's fuel (the extraction reveal) → braiding needs a sixth strand no one can name (the Amara reveal, alongside the Guest-customs chain in [religion.md](religion.md#the-guest-customs)). Let the party *use* the braid for a full arc before anyone asks why it works.

**Vael has no magic.** It cannot cast, make, or break — it has no strand, being the absence the strands were the wall against. Its only powers are perception, patience, and the whisper. Where Vael's attention rests, magic doesn't fail dramatically; it goes *thin* — workings feel lonely, braids come apart quietly, casters doubt the strand will answer (it still does; the doubt is the attack). Anti-magic in this setting is never a field or a null zone. It is always, recognizably, isolation.

**Korrven is not a weaver.** His power is Vael's borrowed perception plus the lattice answering his blood ([History — the Architect's line](history.md#gm-notes)). He cannot braid — he has no one to braid *with*, which is the campaign's thesis in one sentence. His re-tuning of anchors is done with the Guild's stolen craft and his bloodline's authority over the lattice, not spellwork. If the party ever sees him attempt actual magic, it should fail in a way that visibly enrages him.

**Optional (decide at the table): the aspectless braid best.** Amaranth's descendants — Korrven's "misfits," the sorted world's leftovers ([History — his army's secret](history.md#gm-notes)) — often can't hold a single strand at all, and have been called magic-dead all their lives. But in company, braiding, they are naturals: their inheritance *is* the binding. Recommended if a PC or key NPC has Amaranth blood; it foreshadows the "restored keystone as community" ending. Skip it if it would crowd the party's own braid-discovery arc.

**Casters as canaries.** Because casters feel the Hearts' drain directly, weaver NPCs are the best early-warning texture in every kingdom: the Luminari illusionist grieving works that fray, the Veritan adept who taught one student and felt the art leave her hands, the retired Sopharan warder who almost struck once. Use them the way [anchors-and-engines.md](anchors-and-engines.md#gm-notes) uses extraction symptoms — visible wounds before the mechanism is revealed.
