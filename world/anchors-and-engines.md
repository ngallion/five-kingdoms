# The Anchors & the Hearts

**Nature:** The mechanism of the Sundering — and the campaign's arc structure
**Public knowledge:** Almost none. The Hearts are "just engines." The anchors are forgotten.
**See also:** [History](history.md) · [Vael, the Unmade](vael-the-unmade.md)

## The Anchors

Six great works of magic, one sunk beneath each capital, built ~214 years ago to bind the kingdoms together against Vael. Since the sabotaged activation, the lattice runs **inverted**: each anchor binds its kingdom to *itself*. The anchors are why isolation here isn't merely cultural — it is enforced by a spell that is still running. They are also why each kingdom's mastery is uncanny: a ward turned inward concentrates what it encloses.

The keystone — Amaranth's anchor — sits at the center of the **Hollow March**, in the absence where the sixth kingdom used to be.

## The Hearts (Resonance Engines)

Sixty years ago Forgeholm's enginewrights found the anchor sites without understanding them and built a Heart on each — engines that run a kingdom on its own dominant aspect. The catch nobody sees: **the Hearts extract, they don't generate.** Each kingdom is burning its own aspect as fuel, and the proceeds drain down the inverted lattice to Vael. The Hearts are feeding tubes.

Only the **Enginewrights' Guild** of Forgeholm holds the craft of building and tuning them. This is the true prize of Forgeholm — not its weapons.

## State of the Six, Anchor by Anchor

### Luminar — the Heart of a Thousand Doors

**Fuel: completion.** The moment a work is finished, the Heart drinks its significance. This is why nothing in Luminar lasts — no canon, no tradition, only the newest thing. Luminar's compulsive novelty is withdrawal, not temperament.
**Re-tuning needs:** an infusion of *purpose* — Sopharan ethics, something worth finishing a work *for*.

### Veritas — the Silent Heart

**Fuel: shared discovery.** Knowledge spoken aloud is knowledge spent — the Heart drinks the transfer. Veritas's scholars hoard because, without knowing why, they have *felt* their insights dim each time they taught. The suspicion is learned, and it is correct.
**Re-tuning needs:** *fellowship* — Agoran's gift, someone to share with whom the sharing strengthens.

### Forgeholm — the First Heart (the Mother Engine)

**Fuel: the purpose of labor.** The Heart drinks the *why* out of work, leaving only the *how*. The widening gap between those who operate the machines and those operated by them is the extraction made visible.
**Re-tuning needs:** *wisdom* — Sophara's question ("should we?") asked inside the forge, and Agoran's answer to "for whom?"
**Special:** the Mother Engine is the master tap and the Guild's seat. Whoever holds it holds the craft that touches every anchor. **This is why the king is coming.** *(GM: the Mother Engine is not the master tap because it was built first. It is the master tap because of a sealed act of "failed" tampering in 195 AS — Wright Herrek's splice, which tied it into the lattice itself. The Guild doesn't know the splice took; the tamperer's son is the king at the gates. See [Korrven](../characters/npcs/the-king.md).)*

### Sophara — the Still Heart

**Fuel: resolve.** The Heart drinks the moment between decision and act. Sopharan paralysis is not a character flaw — it is literal, ongoing extraction. Their bitterness (knowing the right answer, unable to move on it) is the most honest response in the five kingdoms.
**Re-tuning needs:** *tools and hands* — Forgeholm's practice, Agoran's accountability. Sophara may also be the first kingdom the party can actually heal: give them back one act of resolve and the dam breaks.

### Agoran — the Warm Heart *(now the King's Heart)*

**Fuel: warmth toward strangers.** The Heart drank Agoran's hospitality until only proximity remained — togetherness with nothing holding it together. Into that hollow, the king walked.
**Current state:** Korrven has partially re-tuned this anchor toward *himself* — the first node of his new lattice. Agoran's belonging is real now, and it flows through him.
**Re-tuning needs:** everything. This is the climax, not an early stop.

### The Keystone — Amaranth's anchor, in the Hollow March

Unmade but not gone: an anchor to a kingdom that no longer exists, humming in a city-shaped absence. The lattice cannot be truly repaired — only re-inverted or re-keyed — without confronting it. Vael is at its strongest here. So, perhaps, is what's left of Amaranth.

## What the Party Can Do With an Anchor

Two options per kingdom, and the choice should always hurt:

- **Destroy it.** The extraction stops instantly — and so does the ward. The kingdom loses its uncanny mastery overnight and is thrown, raw and unprepared, into needing everyone else. Fast, violent, honest. Chaos follows.
- **Re-tune it.** Feed the anchor a *different* aspect — which requires a living bearer, relic, or practice from another kingdom, physically present at the Heart. Mechanically and thematically, re-tuning **forces cross-kingdom cooperation**: you cannot heal Luminar without something of Sophara, or Veritas without something of Agoran. Slower, harder, and it holds.

Every anchor re-tuned visibly weakens Korrven (uniting kingdoms is the only thing that can kill him) — and visibly enrages Vael, which will begin working on the party directly.

## The Endgame — the Keystone Problem

Korrven's plan: seize the Guild, re-tune all six anchors, and re-run the Working with **himself as the living keystone** — every kingdom bound to him, a community that cannot exile its king because he *is* the binding.

*(GM: the plan is only possible because of his blood. The keystone's "correction" is written in the Architect's hand, and the lattice answers the line that wrote it — Korrven's line. See [History — GM Notes](history.md#gm-notes).)*

The party's counter is not just stopping him. The lattice wants a keystone. The final choice of the campaign is what — or who — stands in Amaranth's place: a restored keystone of Love (perhaps rebuilt by Amaranth's own unwitting descendants in Korrven's army), the party themselves, or nothing at all — a world that chooses connection freely, unbound, and takes its chances with Vael's patience.

## Who Knows What (GM reference)

- **Common folk:** the Hearts are engines. Full stop.
- **Enginewrights' Guild:** knows the Hearts sit on something older; guards this as a trade secret without grasping it. A few senior wrights have noticed the "fuel" problem and been quietly retired. One went further: **Wright Herrek** traced the draw to the lattice and spliced the Mother Engine into it (195 AS). The Guild ruled the splice a failure and exiled his line; the tribunal is sealed even from most masters. It was not a failure.
- **Veritas archives:** hold the pre-Sundering texts that could explain everything — unread, hoarded, and mostly in the **Concord Tongue**, the inter-kingdom language unmade along with Amaranth ([History — GM Notes](history.md#gm-notes)). (The Vael references already established in [vael-the-unmade.md](vael-the-unmade.md) are far older — a separate, mundanely dead language.)
- **Sophara:** has correctly deduced that the isolation is *enforced* rather than chosen — as a thought experiment, endlessly debated, acted on by no one.
- **Korrven:** knows more than anyone living — Vael has shown him the lattice. He may not know what the bond hides from him — starting with what his own father did to the Mother Engine.
- **The Architect** (if used): knows the full design. The only one who ever did — and Korrven's ancestor ([History — GM Notes](history.md#gm-notes)).

## GM Notes

- The Hearts give every kingdom arc a concrete objective with a location, guardians, and a moral choice — use them as the spine of each "healing quest" (see PLAN.md Phase 3).
- Don't reveal the extraction mechanic all at once. Let the party notice symptoms first (a Luminari artist grieving a masterpiece nobody remembers; a Sopharan who *almost* stood up). The Hearts should feel benign until suddenly they don't.
- Vael's counterattack scales with progress: after the second re-tuning it stops being subtle about isolating the party from each other.
- The extraction has runoff: the March has steeped in sixty years of drained aspect-stuff, and since the strain of 209 it has been breathing the result out into the wilds as the monster surge. [The Hollowing](the-hollowing.md) is the stutter's louder sibling — a second seismograph of the lattice, and the king's engine of consent.
