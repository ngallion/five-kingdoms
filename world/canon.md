# Canon Spine (GM only — spoilers throughout)

The load-bearing facts of the setting, one screen long. **Check new writing against this file instead of re-reading the full lore.** Each entry links to the file that owns the detail. If a change touches anything here, update this file in the same commit.

## Dating & now

- Year 0 = **the Sundering**. The present is **214 AS**. The Hearts were built 152–160 AS; Korrven was exiled 195 AS; Agoran fell (welcomed him) **209 AS** — five years ago. → [history.md](history.md)

## The deep truth

- There were **six** kingdoms. The sixth, **Amaranth** (aspect: **Love**, the binding itself), was *unmade* — erased from land, record, and memory. The Hollow March is where it stood. → [history.md](history.md)
- The Sundering was the **Anchor Working inverted**: six anchors meant to bind the kingdoms together against Vael were sabotaged by **the Architect** (a trusted designer, whispered to by Vael — and Korrven's ancestor), binding each kingdom to *itself* instead. The isolation is a still-running spell, breakable anchor by anchor. → [anchors-and-engines.md](anchors-and-engines.md)
- **The Hearts** (Resonance Engines, built by Forgeholm's Guild atop the buried anchors) *extract*, not generate — each kingdom burns its own aspect as fuel and the proceeds drain to Vael. Each kingdom's signature dysfunction is an extraction symptom. Only the Enginewrights' Guild can build or tune them; that craft is Forgeholm's true prize. → [anchors-and-engines.md](anchors-and-engines.md)
- **Vael, the Unmade**: ancient entity of separation, no force of its own — it works through partners. The Architect was the first; **Korrven is the second**. → [vael-the-unmade.md](vael-the-unmade.md)

## The villain

- **Korrven** was exiled from **Forgeholm** at 195 AS when his father, Wright Herrek of the Mother Engine, was condemned in a sealed tribunal; the splice Herrek was condemned for *succeeded* (it's why the Mother Engine is the master tap). The line descends from the Architect — the lattice answers Korrven's blood. He is beaten by the kingdoms *uniting*, not by violence. → [the-king.md](../characters/npcs/the-king.md)
- His plan: he doesn't need armies — he needs Forgeholm's **enginewrights**, to re-tune anchor after anchor into a lattice that answers him. → [history.md](history.md)

## The systems

- **Magic** is **strandcraft**: aspect-work in six strands. The lattice sorted it into five narrow warped traditions (Brightwork / Exact Art / Runework / Considered Art / Hearthbinding). Braiding strands is the old, lost way — and **all braiding works through Amara**; every party braid quietly feeds her return. → [magic.md](magic.md)
- **Religion**: the **Concord of Six** (Ora, Theora, Ferrun, Sophar, Tavan, and the erased **Amara**), one god per aspect, never worshipped alone — now five warped solo cults. The gods are real, silent since the Sundering, **sealed inside the anchors**; the Hearts burn each aspect *through* its god. Vael survives in folk tales as "the Hollow One." → [religion.md](religion.md)
- **The Hollowing**: the March breathes out march-things (they wear absence, hunt the *isolated*; company is armor). The king's **Wardens** heroically fight the tide his **Dousers** secretly aim — the protection racket that buys consent. The herding proof is a campaign-tier reveal. → [the-hollowing.md](the-hollowing.md)

## The map

- The continent is a **wheel**: Forgeholm north (Ironspine), Veritas east (Tablelands), Sophara south (Meander country), Luminar west (Glassreach coast, the only sea), Agoran northwest (the Granary), all ringing the **Hollow March**. Dead spoke-roads run inward; the **Girdle** rim road links the capitals: **Kaldrum** (Forgeholm), **Meridian** (Veritas), **the Stoa** (Sophara), **Prismere** (Luminar), **Commonhold** (Agoran). → [geography.md](geography.md)

## Campaign shape

- Arc: discover the threat in **Forgeholm** (opens in Kaldrum, at the Wayfair's solstice fair) → rally Sophara / Veritas / Luminar → liberate Agoran → sever/defeat Korrven. Per-kingdom healing = re-tune (or destroy) its anchor; each re-tuning needs an infusion of another kingdom's aspect. → [anchors-and-engines.md](anchors-and-engines.md), [PLAN.md](../PLAN.md)
