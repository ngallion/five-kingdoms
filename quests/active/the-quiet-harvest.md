# The Quiet Harvest

**Status:** Active — running in the background from Session 1; discoverable at any tier
**Type:** Main (cross-arc conspiracy — the thread that runs under all five kingdom arcs)
**Given by:** The pattern itself — missing Wayfarers, vanished dissenters, and one man who came back
**Session introduced:**

## Hook

People are disappearing, and every kingdom has a word that keeps it from being noticed. Forgeholm's wrights are *quietly retired*. Sophara's heretics are *censured* and drift away. Meridian's dark towers *went north*. Agoran's unseated *walk out onto the plains*. And on the Wayfair's circuit, a rigger who misses the morning count has *gone ahead to the next fair* — except Tam Merrival keeps a private tally, and nobody has gone ahead. Since the Kaldrum solstice, two riggers and a lamp-mender are simply *gone*.

Entry points, by arc: the Second Stamp's Ledger of the Retired lists smuggled-out wrights who never reached the fair (Forgeholm); a fork-island exile vanishes from a locked city (Sophara); a faded artist disappears the morning after refusing the Patron (Luminar); the Marginalia's couriers stop answering (Veritas); the Setting-Out ledger and the re-paved Stub sit side by side in Commonhold (Agoran). And somewhere on a border road, the party meets **Fenn** *(see GM Notes)* — the one who came back.

## Objective

*(As the party understands it)* Find where the vanished go — and get them back.

## What's Really Going On

The disappearances are one pipeline with three stages:

1. **Selection.** [The Gleaner](../../characters/npcs/the-gleaner.md) works the margins ahead of and behind the fair, offering the Found's belonging. Her ledger has two columns: those who take the offer, and those who refuse it but *matter* — dissenters with followings, cast-out wrights with craft, and above all the fair's own people. The second column is not abandoned. It is **flagged**.
2. **The taking.** Douser rendition crews — "the quiet wagons," windowless, running the dead spoke-roads by night under the king's own seal — black-bag the flagged and carry them inward. The manifests bypass [Warden-General Harrow](../../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md) entirely; the operation wears his corps' logistics and never touches his ledgers.
3. **The Foundation.** The wagons converge on the staging yard at Commonhold's re-paved Stub and run the rebuilt spoke-road into the Hollow March — to **[the Foundation](../../locations/the-foundation.md)**, the labor camp at the center of the world, where the taken are enslaved to dig down to Amaranth's keystone and raise **the Sixth Engine** over it: the Heart that was never built, made ready for the king's living-keystone Binding.

**Why these victims:** the March unravels kingdom-folk — the lattice binds them to a self the center cannot hold. But it *cannot grip the cast-out*: Wayfarers belong to a kingdom that doesn't exist, and the unseated, censured, struck-off, and scoured have been severed from their kingdom's binding by their own punishments. The kingdoms' cruelties manufacture the one workforce that can survive the site — and the one nobody comes looking for.

**The secret inside the secret:** the Foundation's chief engineer, the bent old prisoner the work-gangs call the Old Wright, is **[Herrek](../../characters/npcs/herrek.md)** — the king's father, alive, enslaved in his son's own great work, and neither of them knows.

## Clues & Evidence

- **Tam's tally** — the circuit's true count vs. Sera's gentle "gone ahead"; the fair's first admission that it is being eaten
- **Fenn's scarf** — the stolen troupe-scarf he escaped under: proof the fair's colors are a *claim* the March respects, and the seed of the breakout plan
- **Manifests without a counter-seal** — warden logistics stamped with the king's seal alone; Harrow's absence from his own paperwork is the crack ([his file](../../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md#gm-notes))
- **The two-column ledger** — the Gleaner's book: recruit / flag. Names the party knows appear in the second column
- **The Setting-Out ledger + the Stub** — Commonhold's short locked drawer and its freshly lit plaza, one discoverable apart ([Commonhold](../../locations/commonhold.md#gm-notes))

## GM Notes

**Pacing.** Tiers 1–2 this is *weather*: an empty bunk, a word compared, Fenn met on a road and forgotten by morning. The party should assemble it sideways while the kingdom arcs run. It goes foreground at Tier 3 — ideally triggered by a taking that lands close (candidates, in ascending cruelty: a fork-island exile, a Marginalia courier, Brindle, Kest Varro, Wick). The Harvest accelerates as the Engine nears completion; takings per season is a dial you can turn to signal the endgame's approach.

**Fenn is evidence that must be carried communally.** The March half-unmade him: alone, he slides out of memory — notes about him blur, sketches don't hold. A party (or a fair) that *keeps him among them* keeps him real: testimony as fellowship, the Reading's mechanic in a single man. If the party ever leaves him somewhere safe and alone, he is gone when they return, and that is their fault to carry.

**The breakout is the campaign's method, staged.** Nobody sneaks out of the Foundation alone — the March eats the lone, and [Cade's whole craft](../../characters/npcs/dovan-cade.md) is keeping the taken isolated in a crowd. You leave *together* or not at all: Brindle's route-song to hold direction, the fair's colors extended to every prisoner (the sixth wagon's claim, made at scale), a braid of every kingdom's aspect to hold the way open, and the Loosening as cover if the Wayfair dares bring the fair to the March's edge. Run it as the Reading's dark twin: one working, many hands, and the length of a song to get everyone out.

**What Vael gets — and what it doesn't know it's assembled.** The camp feeds it twice: the labor of the isolated at its strongest place, and the Engine that will seal its feast. But the Foundation is also the largest gathering of Amaranth's blood since the unmaking — collected, sorted, and delivered to the keystone *by the king himself*. If the party frees them, the population of the campaign's best ending ([a restored Amaranth](../../world/anchors-and-engines.md#the-endgame--the-keystone-problem)) is standing on its own unmade ground, holding hands in the dark. Korrven has accidentally arranged the one thing that can end him. Vael has not noticed. Do not let it notice first.

**Guard the name.** Nobody at the Foundation says "Korrven" — prisoners know only *the king*, and Herrek believes his whole line died in the wilds. The moment Herrek hears his son's name — or the king learns the Old Wright's — is a table-defining scene each way ([staging in his file](../../characters/npcs/herrek.md#gm-notes)). Protect both reveals until they can land at full weight.

**Harrow's hammer.** The herding proof breaks the wardens' faith; the Harvest proof breaks *Harrow* — his corps' wagons, his roads, his seal-shaped absence. Sequence them: herding first (doubt), Harvest second (the break). A Warden-General who has seen the Foundation does not go back to the Musterfields quietly.
