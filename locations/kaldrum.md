# Kaldrum, the Forge-City

**Type:** City — capital of Forgeholm and seat of the Enginewrights' Guild
**Region:** [Forgeholm — the Ironspine](../world/geography.md#forgeholm--the-ironspine-north)
**Status:** Active — the king's Provisioner is inside the walls; **the campaign opens here**
**See also:** [Forgeholm](../world/kingdoms/forgeholm.md) · [The Anchors & the Hearts](../world/anchors-and-engines.md) · [The Wayfair](../world/factions/the-wayfair.md) · [The Second Stamp](../world/factions/the-second-stamp.md)

## Description

You hear Kaldrum before you see it — hammer-choirs, the shriek of cable-lifts, rail-sleds booming down the ore-runs — and under all of it, steady as a pulse, **the hum**. The Mother Engine at the mountain's root sounds through every wall, every floor, every cup of tea in the city. Kaldrumers stop hearing it by their first birthday. Visitors can't sleep for a week. Nobody in living memory has heard it *stop*.

The city is built into the mountain as much as on it: terraces of workshops descending from the wind-scoured surface, through galleries cut ever deeper, to the sealed sanctum at the root. In Kaldrum, **depth is status**. Smoke rises and heat sinks toward the Engine, so the deeper you live, the warmer your rooms, the cleaner your air, and the older your family's marks. The poor live closest to the sky.

## The Shape of the City

- **The Chimneys** *(surface terraces)* — where the great stacks exhale and the rime never quite melts. Home of the **Unmarked**: operators, haulers, sorters — the people who run machines they are forbidden by Guild law to repair or understand. Grey snow, communal steam-kitchens, and more kindness than the Bench believes.
- **The Ladders** *(mid-terraces)* — the working city: workshop rows, certification halls, the rail-yards, the fungus-cellar markets. Every wall stenciled with gauge-marks and safety litanies ([Ferrun's prayers, recited exactly and meaning nothing](../world/religion.md#ferrun-the-shaping-hand-forgeholm--technology)). Cable-lifts run on published schedules that have not slipped in forty years.
- **The Bench** *(deep city)* — the masters' terraces and the **Guildhall**, which stands in what was Ferrun's temple. Guild oaths are still sworn on the forge-altar's anvil, beneath the over-door carving no one reads anymore: *a tool is a promise to the hand that will use it.* Below the Guildhall: the **Deep Archive**, where the Guild keeps everything it certifies — and everything it seals.
- **The Root** *(sealed)* — the Mother Engine, in the mountain's oldest chamber: Ferrun's original sanctum, though no record finds that worth mentioning. Access requires the First Wright's own mark. The wrights who tend it call the duty "keeping the promise," and could not tell you which promise, or to whom.
- **Outside the walls** — the **Made Range**, two centuries of slag-tips grown into artificial hills, and in their lee the **Cinderfield**: the flat, black-cindered common where markets, musters, and — every second or third solstice — [the Wayfair](../world/factions/the-wayfair.md) raise themselves. The fair is camped there *now*.

## Key Features

- **The hum** — the city's involuntary instrument. When the Mother Engine stuttered at the solstice fair, forty thousand people woke like a sleeper whose heart skipped. Everyone in Kaldrum felt it in their teeth; everyone has an opinion about whose fault it was.
- **The Deep Archive** — certification rolls back to the Guild's founding, and behind a sealed door, the tribunal records — including **the Tamperer's Tribunal of 195 AS** and Wright Herrek's confiscated effects ([Forgeholm — GM Notes](../world/kingdoms/forgeholm.md#gm-notes)).
- **The Guildhall anvil** — every wright's oath in Forgeholm's history was sworn on it. Symbolically, whoever administers oaths at that anvil rules the kingdom.
- **The marks system** — certification is citizenship. Your marks say what you may operate, repair, own, and inherit. To be Unmarked is to be, legally, a hand without a name.

## People Here

- **[First Wright Halda Vorn](../characters/npcs/halda-vorn.md)** — chair of the Bench of Masters, keeper of the archive seal; sat as an assessor at the tribunal that exiled the king
- **[Ilsabet Krane](../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md)** — the King's Provisioner, guest of the Guild, bearer of the Agoran Compact
- **[Wrena Calder](../characters/npcs/wrena-calder.md)** — struck-off mender in the Chimneys; keeper of [the Second Stamp](../world/factions/the-second-stamp.md)
- **Master Corwith** — youngest master on the Bench; certifies ventilation and water-works, which takes him to the Chimneys more than his peers like. Quietly funds the Second Stamp and has never once said so aloud.
- **Archivist Pell Lunt** — near-blind keeper of the Deep Archive, who knows every shelf by pace-count and memory. Treats the sealed cabinets as a point of professional honor. The only living person who can find the tribunal record *quickly*.
- **Gatemaster Edda Brumm** — commander of the Girdle-gate. Has waved the Wayfair through a sealed border four times in her career and cannot explain why to the review board currently asking her.

## Atmosphere / Mood

Precision under strain. Kaldrum is proud, punctual, and colder than its forges should allow — a city where everything works and nobody asks what for. Since the stutter, the pride has a crack in it: the one sound that never changes, changed. The Guild wants a culprit. The fair is right outside the walls. And the Provisioner's grain-wagons keep arriving, right on schedule.

## GM Notes

**The hum is your instrument.** Use it the way films use a score: steady when the city believes itself safe, stuttering at story beats. The Mother Engine carries the strain of Korrven's re-tuning of Agoran ([why the stutter happened now](../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#gm-notes)) — every escalation in the wider war can be *heard* in Kaldrum before anyone understands it. The party's own braided magic near the Root makes it skip ([Magic — the Braid](../world/magic.md#at-the-daggerheart-table)), which is either a breadcrumb or an accusation depending on who's listening.

**The archive race.** Three parties want the tribunal record: Krane (standing orders to find and take it), Vorn (who knows exactly where it is and guards it with her own seal), and eventually the party (once they learn the king was born here). Pell Lunt is the hinge — he cannot be bribed, but he *can* be convinced the seal is being abused, and he has noticed Krane's clerks asking about "precedents from the nineties." Staging option: the record leaves the archive mid-arc — by theft, by Vorn moving it, or by fire — and becomes a portable MacGuffin.

**Root access.** Getting to the Mother Engine legally requires the First Wright's mark; illegally, it requires the Second Stamp's knowledge of the old vent-galleries (Wrena's people maintain what the Guild forgot it built). Korrven's whole strategy is to be *invited* down — the Compact is written so that "output standardization" eventually requires his inspectors at the Root.

**If the city falls.** Don't protect Kaldrum from the plot. If the party stalls or the Guild takes the king's protection, Korrven enters his home city as its savior — gates opened, anvil oaths re-sworn to him, Vorn deposed or converted, and the campaign's Tier 2+ turns to liberation instead of defense. The Wayfair scatters ahead of it; the Second Stamp goes under-terrace and becomes the resistance in earnest. Losing here should hurt, not end the game.
