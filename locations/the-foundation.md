# The Foundation ("Nowhere")

**Type:** Labor camp and construction site — the Sixth Engine, rising over Amaranth's keystone at the center of the Hollow March
**Region:** [The Hollow March](../world/geography.md#the-hollow-march-center) — the city-shaped absence
**Status:** Hidden — known to the Dousers, the Gleaner, the Steward, and the king; **the Quiet Harvest ends here**
**See also:** [The Quiet Harvest](../quests/active/the-quiet-harvest.md) · [The Anchors & the Hearts](../world/anchors-and-engines.md#the-endgame--the-keystone-problem) · [The Hearts](../world/the-hearts.md#the-keystone-no-heart-was-ever-built-here) · [Vael](../world/vael-the-unmade.md)

## Description

The wagons roll for days down a road that shouldn't hold — the rebuilt spoke, arrow-straight through country that refuses to be looked at — and stop at the bottom of the world. The taken call it **Nowhere**. The manifests call it **the Foundation**, which is the king's kind of joke: a foundation for the new world, laid in the hole where the old one was unmade.

It is a dig, a camp, and a rising engine-housing, arranged in rings inside the city-shaped absence. There is no wall. There has never needed to be one. The sky is wrong here — light arrives without a sun finding it, distances disagree between one glance and the next — and beyond the work-rings the March goes on in every direction, patient as water. At the center, day and night, the excavation descends toward a sound everyone in the camp can feel in their teeth and nobody discusses: the **hum** of the keystone, like the Mother Engine's, like a struck bell that has not finished ringing in two hundred years, like something underneath asking a question.

## The Shape of the Site

- **The Road-end** — where the rebuilt spoke arrives: wagon-yards, stores, the Douser barracks (the only armed presence; the March itself is the garrison). Wagons come in full. They leave empty.
- **The Tables** — the camp proper: long tables and lamplit halls in a terrible imitation of Commonhold. Every prisoner is fed, named on the Steward's roll, and *seated by assignment* — placements rotated by [Dovan Cade](../characters/npcs/dovan-cade.md) so that no two people share a table long enough to become friends. Warmth, administered; company, prevented. The camp starves its people of the one armor the March respects, and most never work out why they feel so alone in a crowd.
- **The Dig** — the tiered excavation at the absence's center, down through soil that remembers being streets *(the workings expose cellar-lines, hearth-stones, a well — the unmade capital's footprint, which the taken are forbidden to name)*. At the bottom: the keystone's chamber, where only the strongest-blooded gangs can work an hour at a stretch.
- **The Housing** — the Sixth Engine rising over the Dig on scaffolds: a Heart-housing scaled beyond anything in the five capitals, built to the Old Wright's drawings from salvaged pre-Sundering stone. It is beautiful. The wrights among the prisoners hate that it is beautiful.
- **The Quiet Line** — the perimeter nobody drew: the ring where the camp's lamplight fails and the March begins. The Crowd walks it *(see GM Notes)*. Prisoners who cross it alone are not punished. They are simply mourned at the next meal, plates set the old way, by people who never learned they were keeping a Guest custom.

## Key Features

- **The Steward's roll** — Cade's ledger of the taken: name, kingdom, craft, *grip* (his private measure of how well the March tolerates each prisoner). Names are struck through as prisoners die — or fade, the strikethrough growing fainter down the page as the March takes more of them than their lives.
- **The scrip of belonging** — no coin, no whip. The camp's currency is *place*: seat assignments, work-gang postings, lamp-oil, the right to a name spoken aloud. Cade pays and fines in belonging, and the system runs almost without violence, which is the most Korrven thing about it.
- **The second stage** *(GM)* — hidden in the Housing's root, disguised as structural work: [Herrek](../characters/npcs/herrek.md) is building the unfinished half of his repair *into his captors' engine*. The apotheosis machine is being quietly fitted with its own mercy.
- **The wrongness of escape** — space knots for the lone: solo runners walk out of the lamplight and arrive back at the Quiet Line from the other side, hours later, older-eyed. The March returns what it does not keep.

## People Here

- **[Dovan Cade](../characters/npcs/dovan-cade.md)** — Steward of the Foundation: the unseated man who walked the March and lived, keeping the king's camp with terrible gentleness
- **[Herrek, "the Old Wright"](../characters/npcs/herrek.md)** — chief engineer by coercion; the prisoner the work-gangs orbit; the king's father, unknown to everyone including the king
- **Marna Quill** — struck-off Second Stamp mender, taken from the Thornfell fringe; runs the tool-crib and keeps the night queue's habit alive — mends things for prisoners, free, with a blacked thumbprint where her stamp used to be
- **The Concluded** — a censured Sopharan rhetor (he answered one question too publicly) who has begun, very quietly, reading the forbidden hearth-stones' names aloud at table; prisoners request seats near him, which Cade's rotations keep breaking up
- **Liss** — a Wayfair rigger, one of the two taken after the Kaldrum fair; keeps the morning count among the prisoners the way Tam keeps it on the circuit, and is teaching the camp the lullaby, verse by verse, under the work-songs

## Atmosphere / Mood

The politest hell in the world. Fed, named, lamplit, and hopeless — a camp where the cruelty is architectural and the guards are geography, where people are held not by chains but by the certain knowledge that the dark walks in circles. Under it all, always, the hum: the unmade kingdom's anchor, asking its question through the soles of everyone's feet. Some prisoners hate it. More of them, and this frightens the ones who notice, find it *comforting* — like a lullaby they can almost place.

## GM Notes

**Why the camp works — and its one flaw.** The March cannot grip the cast-out, so the workforce survives; the Crowd patrols the Quiet Line, so the lone cannot leave; Cade's rotations keep the prisoners isolated-in-company, so no fellowship forms to armor a group escape. The flaw is the same as the design: **everything here is held apart on purpose, which means everything here is waiting to be braided.** Liss's lullaby, the Concluded's read names, Marna's free mends — three Guest customs already alive in the camp, unnoticed. The breakout ([staging in the quest file](../quests/active/the-quiet-harvest.md#gm-notes)) is those three habits, joined.

**The Crowd at the Line.** The March's worst thing serves as the camp's wall: [the Crowd](../world/the-hollowing.md#the-march-things) — the mob that exists only when you are alone — circles the Quiet Line endlessly. Cade did not arrange this and could not have; it gathered when the camp did, drawn to the greatest concentration of enforced loneliness in the world. Vael's kin, guarding Vael's larder, unbidden. The horror for players: the wall is *fed by the camp it encloses*, and every prisoner Cade isolates makes it thicker.

**What the king knows.** Korrven has visited twice — hooded, unannounced, walking the Dig by night. He has stood within ten feet of the bent old wright whose drawings he approves by courier, and did not look at him: prisoners' faces are a thing he does not permit himself, which he believes is discipline and is actually flinch. **Vael knows exactly who the Old Wright is.** It has never said. A father mended and returned is worse fuel than a father mourned ([the curation](../world/vael-the-unmade.md#what-the-bond-costs-korrven)) — this concealment, once proven, is the sharpest single lever the party ever gets on the bond.

**The hum is not neutral.** The keystone under the Dig is Amara's — *whatever remains of Amara is the hum in the keystone* ([Religion — GM Notes](../world/religion.md#gm-notes)). The prisoners who find it comforting are not wrong; the camp is digging toward the buried god of Love with the last of Love's people, and something down there knows its own. Decide late what that means. Seed it now: the deepest gangs sing better than they should, and Cade's grip-measure improves, inexplicably, with every verse Liss teaches.

**If the party never comes.** The Housing completes around the time Korrven takes (or loses) Forgeholm; after that, only the Guild's craft is missing. An unvisited Foundation doesn't stall — it finishes, and the endgame arrives with the Sixth Engine already built. The Quiet Harvest is on a clock; the takings accelerating is the sound it makes.
