# The Stoa, City of a Thousand Courtyards

**Type:** City — capital of Sophara, seat of the schools and the Colloquy
**Region:** [Sophara — the Meander Country](../world/geography.md#sophara--the-meander-country-south)
**Status:** Active — a refugee camp stands on the disputed fork; **the Sophara arc centers here**
**See also:** [Sophara](../world/kingdoms/sophara.md) · [The Anchors & the Hearts](../world/anchors-and-engines.md) · [The Closed Hand](../world/factions/the-closed-hand.md) · [The Wayfair](../world/factions/the-wayfair.md)

## Description

You know the Stoa by what's missing — the river makes no sound. Through the whole capital the Meander lies glass-still, mirror-flat, silent between its banks; boatmen pole across water that never ripples, and visitors lie awake listening for a current that never comes. Sopharans say the water is "considerate." It is the **Stillworks** *(see GM Notes)*. Over the silence, the city murmurs: ten thousand arguments running at once through linked courtyards, colonnades, and debate-gardens, a sound like rain that never lands.

Nothing in the Stoa is ever finished being discussed, so nothing is ever quite final: streets change names depending on which school's map you bought, staircases stop mid-flight pending appeal, and the great Colloquy Hall has stood roofed but formally "unaccepted" for a hundred and forty years.

## The Shape of the City

- **The Two Banks** — the city proper, spread along both branches of the fork. Each bank's schools hold that *their* branch is the true Meander; each calls its own side the True Bank; the farmers, who water their fields from both, call them East Bank and West Bank and get on with it. School-rows, lecture-courts, the stepped acoustic gardens called the Listening Steps.
- **The Argument** *(the fork island)* — the oldest debate-garden in the world and [Sophar's temple](../world/religion.md#sophar-the-weigher-sophara--philosophy), on the island where the river divides: ground claimed by both banks for four hundred years and governed by neither. At the temple's heart, the scale-altar balanced to touch nothing. On the island's flat southern half: the Forgeholm-built flood-engine everyone calls the Stillworks — and, since this summer, **the camp**: forty hearths of Agoran exiles under patched canvas, landed by night barge and legally untouchable.
- **The Stub** — the festival-sized plaza at the dead end of the northern spoke-road, built for pilgrim crowds that stopped coming 214 years ago. Grass in the paving, market stalls at one edge. Big enough for a fair *(the Wayfair knows)*.
- **The Hall of the Pending** — the archive of every question awaiting the Colloquy, shelved in order of filing, tended by the Provisional's clerks with genuine devotion. A petition filed today enters the queue somewhere past ninety thousand. Nothing has ever left it.
- **The Granges** — the terraced valley beyond the walls: vineyards, seed-halls, the barge-quays. The farmers finish things, feed everyone, and have been quietly ferrying Tam Merrival's passengers for years.

## Key Features

- **The still water** — the city's involuntary instrument, as the hum is Kaldrum's. When the Stillworks falters, the Meander *shows current* — and since the camp landed, there are ripples some mornings. Everyone has an opinion about what that means. Nobody's opinion is close.
- **The Quorum Bell** — cast at the founding to summon the Colloquy, hanging in the unaccepted Hall. It has not rung in two centuries; the rope is ceremonially renewed every year by clerks who consider the job an honor. Students dare each other to touch it.
- **The Pending** — 213 years of unanswered questions in physical form. Anyone may file; no one is heard. The Closed Hand has begun reading shelved petitions *aloud in public*, oldest first, and the readings draw crowds that go very quiet.
- **The scale-altar** — Sophar's scale, balanced to touch nothing. Every Sopharan feels a bitterness in front of it they do not examine ([Religion](../world/religion.md#sophar-the-weigher-sophara--philosophy)).

## People Here

- **[Prolocutor Casta Meren](../characters/npcs/casta-meren.md)** — keeper of the agenda; presides over nothing, rules everything that matters
- **[Ereni Thole](../characters/npcs/ereni-thole.md)** — the retired warder; her wards ring the camp, cast free of charge, holding better than any she has ever set
- **[Kest Varro](../characters/npcs/kest-varro.md)** — the censured student; signs everything *Kest Varro, Concluded*
- **[Maro Tallis](../characters/npcs/maro-tallis.md)** — the '09 exile everyone trusts; rooms on the West Bank, and lately avoids the camp
- **First Steward Verch** — head of the Provisional; two hundred years of interim authority end the day the Bell rings, and he knows it. Courteous, tireless, and orthodoxy's actual spine.
- **Keeper Ashwel** — old priest of Sophar, tends the temple garden and the altar. Leaves bread at the camp before dawn and tells no one. Has begun to suspect his god's scale was built to come to rest.
- **Grange-Speaker Ambel Tors** — the valley's voice. Was carrying refugees before the schools knew refugees existed; feeds the camp openly and dares anyone to file a complaint. Holds the water-records that could settle the river question in an afternoon.
- **Hearthmother Bry** — the camp's center: an Agoran matriarch who lost her table once and will not lose another. The camp's long table is open to *any* Sopharan who sits — the destitute feeding the wealthy — and those who eat there find small decisions coming easier on the walk home *(see GM Notes)*.

## Atmosphere / Mood

A city holding its breath — and just now noticing how long it has held it. The Stoa is gracious, unhurried, and lit like late afternoon all day; under the courtesy, the bitterness of people who know the answer and cannot say it. Since the camp landed, something has shifted: the arguments in the courtyards have an edge the schools can't file down, there is chalk on the statues, and some mornings, the river moves.

## GM Notes

**The island is the anchor site.** The Still Heart lies beneath the Argument — the fork island is why the temple is there, and the Stillworks was built on it sixty years ago by enginewrights who found the site without understanding it. Its public face is flood-control: it drinks resolve and stills the river, and the mirror-water everyone finds so restful is the extraction made visible. **The camp is pitched directly on the anchor.** Tam chose the island for its jurisdiction; whether something older helped him choose is yours to decide. Either way the arithmetic is delicious: an Agoran community — fellowship, accountability, the long table — is living on the Still Heart's lid, and the ripples in the river are the anchor straining to digest an aspect it was never tuned for. Bry's suppers genuinely loosen people. The re-tuning has, in miniature, already begun; nobody knows.

**The jurisdiction trap.** No writ can touch the camp until the Colloquy decides which branch is the true river — and no motion has been heard in 213 years, because of the Deferral, whose docket the Closed Hand is demanding be resumed. Every legal road out of the crisis runs through the first Verdict since 1 AS. The trap works on the party's enemies too: Maro cannot get the camp cleared quietly. He needs the Verdict to happen and go *his* way — which is why the movement's own organizer will become the motion's strangest ally.

**Staging the Verdict.** Midwinter: the Wayfair raises its fair on the Stub — pilgrim-festival ground doing its old job for the first time in 214 years — and the Loosening opens the one window in which Sopharans can act. Build the scene in layers: the Myriad's show, the crowd walking from the fairgrounds to the Hall still warm with it, the rope nobody has pulled in two centuries, and the question of **who rings the Quorum Bell**. Options for the dam-break act of resolve the anchors file promises: Ereni's first strike in forty years, cast in the camp's defense; Meren calling the question herself; or a PC's hand on the rope. When the Bell rings, the river should *move*.

**If it goes wrong.** Maro's version of the Verdict ends with Sophara accepting the king's protection *provisionally* — and his fallback, if unmasked too late, is the ambush: a run of Tam's taken on the Fens causeway, names traveled north, the camp blamed for its own tragedy. Losing the Stoa doesn't look like occupation; it looks like a signed motion, in beautiful hand, with Meren's seal regretfully affixed.
