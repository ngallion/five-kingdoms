# Commonhold, the City of Tables

**Type:** City — capital of Agoran, seat of the king
**Region:** [Agoran — the Granary](../world/geography.md#agoran--the-granary-northwest)
**Status:** The king's seat — orderly, grateful, and open to anyone; **the campaign's climax stages here**
**See also:** [Agoran](../world/kingdoms/agoran.md) · [Korrven](../characters/npcs/the-king.md) · [The Found](../world/factions/the-found.md) · [The Eleventh Chair](../world/factions/the-eleventh-chair.md) · [The Anchors & the Hearts](../world/anchors-and-engines.md)

## Description

You know Commonhold by what it doesn't have — walls. The capital of the fallen kingdom lies open to the plains on every side: a hundred villages grown together into one sprawl of lanes, garden-plots, and great timber ring-barns, every window lit at dusk, every door unlocked, every chimney fed from the same flame. A stranger walking in on any road is met within half a mile — not by guards, by *welcome*: fed at the first table they pass, asked their name, given a bed. By morning, everyone in the quarter knows the name. By the next evening, so does the [Hall of Names](#the-shape-of-the-city).

At the city's heart burns the **Common Hearth** — the great engine-flame at the center of the old moot-ring, from which every hearth-fire in Agoran is lit by custom, mother-flame to a kingdom. It has burned taller these last five years than in living memory. And it *leans*. Whatever the wind does, the flame stands crooked, bending gently and permanently toward the head of the king's table. Agorans say the fire is paying its respects. Nobody finds this alarming. That is the most alarming thing in the city.

## The Shape of the City

- **The Ring** — the great moot-ring where all of Agoran once gathered at harvest, now where the king holds audience: concentric turf benches around the Common Hearth, and the head table where Korrven sits. He built no palace and takes no throne — he sits at the long tables like anyone, in the seat that was always kept empty *(see GM Notes)*. The harvest moots still happen. They are wonderful. Attendance is not compulsory, and is total.
- **The Hundred Tables** — the grown-together villages, each keeping its own ring-barn, its own long table, its own roll of names. An address in Commonhold is not a street; it is *which table feeds you*. The table-song at dusk rises from a hundred halls at once — and every hall now sings the same grace, which old people privately find stranger than any march-thing.
- **The Granaries** — the shared granaries that taught Agoran cooperation, now the largest they have ever been. Grain-trains roll out east along the Girdle under warden escort: the [Agoran Compact](../characters/npcs/ilsabet-krane.md#secrets)'s deliveries, feeding Forgeholm early and to the letter.
- **The Musterfields** — the wardens' grounds on the eastern edge: drill-yards, remount herds, the beacon-yard where the border signal-lines terminate, and the **Survey house**, where route-craft is planned and the beacon ledgers are filed *(see GM Notes)*.
- **The Hall of Names** — the rolls of belonging: every soul in Agoran entered at a table, visited on their naming-day, missed when absent. The gentlest census in the world, and the most complete. [First Steward Iveth Kall](../characters/npcs/iveth-kall.md) keeps it. The Hall's other book is kept in a locked drawer and is very short: the ledger of **the Setting-Out**.
- **The Stub** — like every capital, Commonhold has its festival-plaza at the dead end of the old spoke-road, southwest of the Ring. Under Korrven it has been re-paved and re-lit. Nobody can say what for. *(He can.)*

## Key Features

- **The leaning flame** — the Common Hearth is the city's involuntary instrument, as the hum is Kaldrum's and the still water is the Stoa's. It leans toward the king; on rare nights it gutters or stands suddenly straight, and the whole Ring goes quiet without knowing why *(see GM Notes)*.
- **The long tables and the one grace** — meals are communal, generous, and genuinely warm; grace is said in the king's name, in the cadences of [Tavan's old liturgy](../world/religion.md#tavan-the-long-table-agoran--community). The empty chair survives at every table. What it means is changing.
- **The rolls of belonging** — no one in Agoran goes unfed, unnamed, or unnoticed. Visitors are welcomed onto a table's roll within a day. It takes most guests a week to realize they cannot think of a way to be *alone* here, and another week to realize what that means.
- **The Setting-Out** — Agoran's only punishment. No prisons, no gallows: the condemned is struck from every roll, and a full plate is set outside the door — fed like the guest, never again inside. No one speaks to the unseated; not from hatred, from *sorrow*, which is worse. Most walk out onto the plains within a month. In the age of [the Hollowing](../world/the-hollowing.md), everyone understands what the roads do to the cut-off. The king's justice has never executed anyone.
- **The safest roads in the world** — five years of the Hollowing and not one Agoran village lost. The doors stand open because nothing comes. Agorans read this as proof the king protects his own; a player with a map can read it as [something else](../world/the-hollowing.md#gm-notes).

## People Here

- **[Korrven](../characters/npcs/the-king.md)** — the king, at the head table; in residence between campaigns, and never announced — he is simply *there*, refilling someone's cup, remembering their child's name
- **[First Steward Iveth Kall](../characters/npcs/iveth-kall.md)** — keeper of the Hall of Names and mistress of the feasts; the beloved administrator of the whole warm machine
- **[Warden-General Bram Harrow](../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md)** — commander of the King's Wardens, at the Musterfields when not riding circuit; the most trusted man in five kingdoms
- **Keeper Damet** — old priest of Tavan who tends the Ring and says the blessings. Served under the old grace for thirty years; says the new one beautifully. Sometimes, alone, he catches himself saying the old name, and stands very still afterward.
- **Beacon-Clerk Noll Wray** — the Survey house's mousy ledger-keeper, proud of his files' perfection. Every route-order the [Dousers](../world/the-hollowing.md#gm-notes) have ever ridden under has passed across his desk, correctly stamped. He has never once read one for meaning.
- **Widow Corren** — keeps a table in the northern lanes; feeds wardens, drovers, and any stranger going. Sets the guest's plate the *old* way and says nothing about it. Two sons in grey cloaks. The quietest hearth of [the Eleventh Chair](../world/factions/the-eleventh-chair.md) inside the capital.
- **"Uncle" Petch** — one of the original Found: a huge, gentle, fingerless veteran of the Thornfell camps who minds children during the feasts and would die for the king without noticing the question. Meant to make the party's stomach hurt.

## Atmosphere / Mood

The friendliest city in the world, and the most watched — and the watching *is* the friendliness; there is no seam between them. Nothing is hidden in Commonhold because nothing needs to be: the surveillance is love, the census is care, the curfew is a lantern carried to your door by someone worried you'd miss supper. Visitors sleep better here than anywhere in the five kingdoms, and wake less certain of things they were certain of. The horror of Commonhold is not that the warmth is false. It is that it is real.

## GM Notes

**The anchor is under the Ring.** Tavan's temple was never a building — it was the moot-ring and the practice itself ([Religion](../world/religion.md#tavan-the-long-table-agoran--community)) — and the Warm Heart sits beneath it. The Common Hearth is the Heart's public face, built sixty years ago over the moot's ancient fire-pit by enginewrights who found the site without understanding it: its flame heats the ring-barns and bakehouses, drives the great mills, and mothers every fire in Agoran. For sixty years it drank warmth-toward-strangers and Agorans felt the feasts go through the motions. Since 209, [Korrven's partial re-tuning](../world/anchors-and-engines.md#agoran--the-warm-heart-now-the-kings-heart) has the flame burning taller than living memory — **Agorans experienced the Fall as a revival**: the winter the fire came back. That is why the gratitude is bottomless, and why every [hearthbinding circle now closes toward him](../world/magic.md#agoran--hearthbinding). The lean is the re-tuning made visible.

**The flame is a seismograph.** Like the Mother Engine's stutter and the Stoa's ripples, the Common Hearth registers the lattice: when the party re-tunes an anchor anywhere in the world, the flame *straightens* for a breath — and Korrven, wherever he is, feels it. If the Wayfair or a braiding party ever works near Commonhold, the flame gutters. Iveth Kall keeps a private log of the flame's bad nights. So, unread, does Noll Wray's beacon-yard. The two logs, side by side, are a discoverable.

**The chair he sits in.** Korrven took the head table's *empty chair* — the guest's seat, Amara's seat ([Religion — GM Notes](../world/religion.md#gm-notes)). Agorans find it humble: the king who takes no throne, who sits where anyone may sit. Theologically it is the whole endgame in furniture: a man rehearsing the keystone, seated where the erased god sat. Any Wayfair elder, any Splendor healer, any recovered scrap of the old liturgy can teach the party to see the chair correctly — and once seen, the image should never leave them.

**The door is open — that is the trap.** Agoran seals nothing. The party can walk into Commonhold on any road, any tier, and be welcomed, fed, and entered on a roll by nightfall. Perfect hospitality is perfect surveillance: they will never be stopped, only *known* — their habits, their kindnesses, their wounds, all of it flowing up through the Hall of Names to a king whose whole art is [the wound](../characters/npcs/the-king.md#he-knows-the-wound). Early visits should feel easy and cost them later. Leaving unremarked is the hard part; the Eleventh Chair's drover-paths and Widow Corren's table are the quiet ways out.

**The Setting-Out is the Dousers' seed-craft.** The unseated are channels of cut connection walking out onto the plains — and the quiet corps has learned to time and *place* Setting-Outs the way it douses beacons, carving the drive-lanes that keep the tide off Agoran ([The Hollowing — GM Notes](../world/the-hollowing.md#gm-notes)). The kingdom's one punishment and its miraculous safety are the same mechanism. This is the darkest discoverable in Agoran; save it for when the party can bear it. And it has a darker floor: not every unseated walks into the tide — the useful ones are *collected*. The quiet wagons stage at the re-lit Stub, and the re-paved spoke-road runs where all spoke-roads run ([the Quiet Harvest](../quests/active/the-quiet-harvest.md)).

**Staging the climax.** Liberation cannot be a sack — the people are not captives, they are *converts*, and [the anchor needs everything](../world/anchors-and-engines.md#agoran--the-warm-heart-now-the-kings-heart). Build toward one night at the Ring with every lever the campaign has loaded: the herding proof breaking the wardens ([Harrow's ledger](../characters/npcs/bram-harrow.md#gm-notes)), the tribunal record breaking the king's story, [Oren Brandt](../characters/npcs/oren-brandt.md) saying the old grace where the flame leans, bearers of every kingdom's aspect at the Hearth, and [Iveth Kall](../characters/npcs/iveth-kall.md#gm-notes) — the voice of the rolls — deciding which grace Commonhold sings. When the flame stands straight and the lean is gone, Agoran is free, whatever Korrven does next.
