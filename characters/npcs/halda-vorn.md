# Halda Vorn, First Wright

**Role:** Ruler of Forgeholm — chair of the Bench of Masters; obstacle, then hinge
**Location:** The Guildhall, [Kaldrum](../../locations/kaldrum.md)
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the opening arc)*
**Status:** Alive, in power, under pressure

## Appearance

A tall woman in her sixties, forge-scarred to the elbows, hair cropped to a grey gauge-line. Wears working leathers with the First Wright's chain — every link a different alloy, one per certified craft — and a master's caliper on a cord, which she uses in conversation the way other people use eye contact. Nothing about her is decorative. Nothing about her is careless.

## Personality

Vorn believes in **measurement** the way clergy believe in gods: the world is knowable, tolerances are honest, and a thing either holds or it doesn't. She is incorruptible by money or flattery — and deeply corruptible by *order*, because order is the thing she cannot bear to lose. She asks *does it work* by reflex and has spent sixty years never once asking *what is it for*, which makes her the perfect First Wright of [Ferrun the Exacting](../../world/religion.md#ferrun-the-shaping-hand-forgeholm--technology)'s Forgeholm, and exactly the ruler Korrven's method was built for.

Her one law of argument, known to every apprentice: *bring me a number.* Show her a measurement she cannot refute and she will follow it anywhere — against the Bench, against the Compact, against her own past. This is how the party wins her. It is also how [Krane](ilsabet-krane.md) very nearly does.

## Backstory

Chimneys-born, which she never mentions and never forgets — she earned every mark without a family line behind her. In 195 AS, as the tribunal's youngest assessor, she examined the graft Wright Herrek had fused into the Mother Engine's root and delivered the finding that made her career: **dead metal — the splice failed.** The verdict was unanimous, the tribunal sealed, the line exiled, and the matter closed. She has cited that finding for nineteen years as proof the system works: even a master of the Mother Engine is not above the gauge.

She was measuring honestly. She was also wrong ([the splice took](../../world/anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine)) — and everything Forgeholm now faces walks in through the error.

## Relationship to the Party

The gatekeeper of everything the party needs in Kaldrum: the Deep Archive seal, Root access, the Guild's judgment on the fair. She has no patience for outlanders and less for mystics — but she is fair to a fault, keeps her word to the letter, and is one honest measurement away from being the party's most powerful ally in the north.

## Secrets

- She holds the only seal to the tribunal record, and re-reads no sealed record, ever — sealed means *finished*.
- She was on shift as a junior when the founding survey's copies of the Engine's draw-figures were archived; she has always known the Heart's output arithmetic doesn't quite sum, and has filed the discrepancy under *instrument error* for forty years. ([Casters as canaries](../../world/magic.md#gm-notes) — this is her version.)
- She signed the "quiet retirement" orders for three senior wrights who asked what the Hearts burn — including [Ondrel Vasht](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#key-members). She believes retirement was mercy.
- She does not know the tamperer's exiled son and the king in Agoran are the same man. When she learns his name, she will go very still, and then she will go to the archive.

## GM Notes

**Her arc is the tribunal's arc.** Vorn's certainty is the load-bearing wall of Forgeholm's story about itself. The campaign's best version of her: the party (likely via Ondrel Vasht, the one living witness who can read the record with modern eyes) confronts her with proof the splice *took* — that her honest measurement was wrong, that the verdict made the king, and that the "failed" graft is why the Mother Engine is the master tap. Played right this is not a gotcha but a conversion: *bring me a number* cuts both ways, and a Vorn who has re-measured is relentless on the party's side.

**How Krane works her.** Not bribery — *specifications*. The [Agoran Compact](ilsabet-krane.md) is engineered to Vorn's values: exact tonnages, generous tolerances, direction for idle forges, and clause by clause, the king's inspectors closer to the Root. After the stutter, Krane offers the thing Vorn wants most — a *culprit that restores order* — and the fair is standing right there. Vorn presiding over a tribunal of the Wayfair, nineteen years after the tribunal that made the king, is the arc's centerpiece scene if you can get it: same hall, same anvil, and the party arguing where no one argued last time.

**The empty chair she keeps.** Guild regulation requires the blank second stamp on every certified work ([the Guest customs](../../world/religion.md#the-guest-customs)); Vorn, who follows all regulations, stamps it without ever once having asked why. If the party ever asks her *what the blank die is for*, let her open her mouth to answer and find nothing there. Rattling her twice — once about the splice, once about the stamp — is how the setting's two deep secrets first brush against the campaign's most rigorous mind.

**If she breaks the wrong way.** Under enough disorder she will take the king's protection to save the city — sincerely, provisionally, and lawfully. Vorn collaborating is worse than Vorn deposed: she will make the occupation *work*. Keep her salvageable; her re-measurement can come as late as the liberation arc.
