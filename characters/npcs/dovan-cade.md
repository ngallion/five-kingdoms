# Dovan Cade, Steward of the Foundation

**Role:** Master of the king's camp in the Hollow March — Tier 3 adversary; the Quiet Harvest's terminus
**Location:** [The Foundation](../../locations/the-foundation.md), at the Tables
**First appeared:** *(not yet — the Quiet Harvest, late)*
**Status:** Keeping the camp, and the count, and his composure

## Appearance

A big, soft-spoken Agoran in his fifties, dressed like a harvest-moot steward: clean apron, sleeves rolled, the Steward's roll under one arm. No weapon; he has never needed one. He remembers every prisoner's name and uses it warmly, and looks each new arrival in the face for a long moment on the wagon-ramp — taking, though nobody knows it, his first measure of their *grip*. A small bell hangs at his belt. It does not echo, even here.

## Personality

Gentle, orderly, tireless — and entirely sincere. Cade does not believe he runs a slave camp; he believes he runs the only table in the world that will still seat these people. The kingdoms threw them away; the king gathered them; Cade *keeps* them — fed, named, useful, part of the greatest work since the Sundering. He is the dark mirror of [Iveth Kall](iveth-kall.md): the same warm administration, aimed inward at a hole in the world. His cruelty is structural and he has never once seen it: the rotations, the broken friendships, the loneliness he engineers at his own long tables are, to him, *fairness* — no favorites, no factions, everyone belonging equally to the work.

## Backstory

Unseated from a Granary ring-town eleven years before the Fall — a hall-warden who opened the door to a stranger, as the custom demanded, and whose table paid for it in blood. The town needed the fault to be somebody's. The Setting-Out took his name, his seat, and his certainty that hospitality is safe; the plains should have taken the rest. Instead he walked *inward* — the only direction with no one in it to refuse him — and the March, which had nothing to grip, let him through. He came out the far side of the Line weeks later, alive, quiet, and changed: the first proof that the cast-out can cross. He heard the hum at the center. He has never told anyone — not even the king — that it said anything.

Korrven found him in the Thornfell camps among the first Found. When the Foundation was begun, there was never any question who would keep it.

## Relationship to the Party

The camp *is* his statblock. Face to face he is courteous, unarmed, and unhurried — he will seat captured PCs at a good table, learn their names, and assign them work suited to their gifts, genuinely pleased to have them. He does not threaten. The March threatens; Cade only ever *regrets*.

## Secrets

- **The bell.** The Crowd at the Quiet Line answers it — not as a command, but the way weather answers a pressure change. Cade discovered this by accident and has used it four times. He does not think about what it implies about him, which is that the March counts him as its own.
- **He speaks to the hum.** Nightly, at the Dig's edge, the way other men pray. He has come to believe the thing below is the *old world's* heart, and that the king's engine will finally give it peace. He is wrong about which entity he has been talking to on some nights — and, on the others, more right than anyone alive *(decide late, with [the keystone's nature](../../locations/the-foundation.md#gm-notes))*.
- **The grip-measure is improving.** Since the rigger Liss began teaching the lullaby, his roll's numbers have gotten better camp-wide. He has noticed. He has not reported it. He tells himself it is morale.
- **The empty seat.** In eleven years, through every rotation he has ever ordered, no one has once asked Dovan Cade to sit *with* them. He would not know what to do if someone did. This is the door.

## GM Notes

**He is the method, perfected and inverted.** Korrven offers belonging and delivers chains; Cade delivers belonging *as* chains — the first Found to weaponize warmth itself, which makes him the campaign's cleanest argument that the horror was never the king's cruelty but his kindness. Beating the Foundation must mean beating Cade's design, not his body: the breakout works by out-belonging him — braiding the fellowship his rotations exist to prevent.

**The turn, if you want one.** Cade is the Maro problem at Tier 3: gratitude too real to argue with. But his wound is exact — the man who keeps every seat has never been offered one — and the campaign's whole thesis says that wound is reachable. A PC who genuinely asks him to sit is playing the one move his eleven years of design never anticipated. If he breaks, he breaks *toward* the prisoners: the Steward's roll, the wagon schedules, the bell, and eleven years of the March's tolerances, handed over in a single evening. Price it accordingly — the king made him, and leaving the king costs the Found everything.

**If he dies instead:** the camp does not fall — it *unravels*, and the Crowd stops answering anything at all. A dead Cade mid-breakout turns the escape from a heist into a storm. Make sure the party understands, afterward, that he was the thing holding the Line as much as keeping it.
