# Vess Tallow, the Finisher

**Role:** The scandal of Prismere — the artist whose finished works *last*; the one maker the king wants above all others
**Location:** A workshop in the Fade, [Prismere](../../locations/prismere.md)
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the Luminar arc)*
**Status:** Alive, unfashionable, and under a standing offer she has not yet refused

## Appearance

Fifties, broad-handed, paint to the elbows, dressed like a dockhand among peacocks and clearly past caring. Moves slowly in a fast city and looks at things for a long time — long enough that people near her get uncomfortable, then curious, then find themselves looking too. Her only ornament is a glass pendant with a stranger's maker's-mark in it, worn facing outward *(see Secrets)*.

## Personality

Blunt, patient, and quietly ungoverned by every rule that runs Luminar. She debuts nothing, competes for nothing, and answers the Season's whole economy of acclaim with a shrug that infuriates the Bill more than any rivalry could. What she has instead of ambition is a craftsman's tenderness for *use*: she wants her work stood on, walked past daily, worn smooth. Her manifesto, when pressed, is eleven words: **"A work is finished when it's given. Everything else is rehearsal."**

The tenderness has a floor of grief under it. She is not serene; she is someone who lost the thing her whole world runs on and built something else in the crater.

## Backstory

She was the Bill's darling once — debut-track at nineteen, and at twenty-six she took the Gasp itself with *Vesperlight*, a painted glass colonnade that made grown Luminaries weep. By the next evening she could stand inside her masterpiece and feel nothing at all — and so could everyone else ([the Heart drinks completion](../../world/anchors-and-engines.md#luminar--the-heart-of-a-thousand-doors); she is the anchors file's grieving artist, the wound made flesh). The city called it the Morning After and moved on. She didn't. She spent a year in the Fade among the other drained wonders, trying to remember why her own work had mattered — and noticed, in that graveyard of masterpieces, the handful of pieces that *hadn't* wholly died: a bridge-rail carved for a ferryman, a painted door made for a wedding. Small things. Given things.

She has spent twenty years testing what she found. She finishes every work now — openly, indecently — but always *for someone*, by name: a mural for the fisher-widows called *The Nets Come Home*, a stairway painted for a school, lamps for the night-crews of the sea-gates. And she signs them with the recipient's mark in the collaborator's corner — filling the blank space every Luminari knows is terrible luck to fill ([Religion](../../world/religion.md#the-guest-customs)). Her works last. People remember them, return to them, grieve them when the salt takes them. The Bill calls her a decorator. The Fade calls her the Finisher. [The Encore](../../world/factions/the-encore.md) guards her murals like relics and has never told her.

## Relationship to the Party

The method, walking. Everything the arc needs — the craft of purposeful completion, the proof that finishing can be survived, the liturgy the re-tuning will use — lives in her hands, and she will teach it to anyone who asks the way an apprentice asks: by working. She is also the arc's most contested ground, because [Marlow Pitch](marlow-pitch.md) has been courting her for two years with the one thing she cannot shrug at.

## Secrets

- **The pendant.** The mark in the glass belongs to Corin Dale, the glasswright she loved and worked beside for a decade — the first person she ever finished a piece *for*. He drowned in a Fens crossing five years ago, in the first season the marsh-lights began taking people ([the Hollowing](../../world/the-hollowing.md) hunts the isolated; he was traveling alone, to fetch her a color). Every corner she fills is his corner first. She has never said this to anyone.
- She has worked out most of the mechanism by observation alone: *finished things die here unless they are given* — and she has begun to suspect the dying is done by something, not decreed by nature. She lacks only the word "Heart." One honest conversation ([Ondrel Vasht](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#key-members) would do it in an evening) turns her craft into the campaign's method.
- **The standing offer.** Pitch's Commission of the Lasting: monuments to the Found in Commonhold — *your work, at scale, meaning something, forever*. She has refused nothing and accepted nothing for two years. What holds her is not temptation to serve the king; it is that the offer is the only voice in twenty years that has called her an artist.
- *(Optional, per [magic.md](../../world/magic.md#gm-notes):)* her strandcraft alone has always been strangely weak — Brightwork barely answers her — but anything made *with* or *for* another hand comes out uncanny. If you want Amaranth blood at the table before the endgame, it is hers.

## GM Notes

**Her hinge is being asked to teach.** Praise bounces off her; commissions bore her; the Gasp she has already survived. What reaches her is a person — ideally a PC — asking to *learn*: the spark treated as a gift in transit, one more time, passing through her on its way to someone else ([Ora's oldest teaching](../../world/religion.md#ora-the-first-spark-luminar--creativity)). The scene where she takes an apprentice is the scene where Luminar's healing becomes possible; play it small and let it land.

**How Pitch works her:** never pressure — *witness*. He attends to her work with total, informed seriousness; he remembers *Vesperlight*; he speaks of Corin Dale with perfect gentleness (his file on her is thorough). Every visit is the world saying *you matter* in the king's voice. Counter him not by argument but by out-witnessing him — the Encore going public with twenty years of kept devotion is the reveal that breaks his spell: she was never unseen. She was beloved by people too unfashionable to count.

**At the Finale,** her part is the craft: the ending given, by name, to the city — corners filled at scale. Whether she stands on the stage or below at the Heart, the completion that re-tunes the anchor is *made her way*, and the campaign should say so out loud.

**If she goes north:** the king gains what no tyrant has ever had — iconography that does not fade — and the party meets her again in Commonhold, celebrated at last, making eternal monuments to the man whose engine killed her masterpiece. Leave her salvageable: what turned her can turn her back, and the Encore keeps her murals dusted against the day she comes home.
