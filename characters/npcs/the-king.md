# Korrven

**Role:** Primary villain, conquering king
**Based in:** Agoran (the Kingdom of Community)
**Status:** Alive, rising in power — currently eyeing Forgeholm

## The Core of Him

He was exiled. Cast out from the kingdom of belonging by the very people he wanted to be part of. That wound never healed — it became a furnace. He spent years in the margins, finding others like him: misfits, monsters, cast-outs. He gave them what he always wanted. A home. A place where they belonged.

And then he went back.

He does not rule through fear alone. He gives people something real — purpose, identity, a place to belong. The people of Agoran are genuinely grateful to him. That is the horror of it.

## His Power

*Korrven's power flows from his bond with **[Vael, the Unmade](../../world/vael-the-unmade.md)** — an entity of primordial loneliness older than the five kingdoms. The bond is not possession. It is alignment. Their goals are the same.*

### He Feeds on Isolation

Every sundering between people — every exile, every closed border, every kingdom that turns inward — he feels it and grows stronger. The five kingdoms' centuries of isolation have been feeding him, slowly, for a long time. He may not have caused their isolation, but he has learned to cultivate it.

When the kingdoms draw apart, he grows. When they come together, he weakens. The heroes uniting the kingdoms is not just strategy — it is the only thing that can actually kill him. Violence alone won't do it.

### He Knows the Wound

He has an uncanny, almost preternatural sense for what people lack — what a kingdom has been starved of, what an individual is desperate for. This is not manipulation exactly. He is genuinely perceptive, genuinely empathetic in a dark way. He *understands* loneliness because he lived it.

When he approaches a kingdom, he offers exactly what it needs. His offer is always real. His version is always poisoned.

- **To Luminar** he offers meaning — *your creations will serve a great purpose, my purpose, and they will last forever*
- **To Veritas** he offers collaboration — *your discoveries will be shared, studied, built upon — by my scholars, in my service*
- **To Forgeholm** he offers direction — *tell me what to build and I will tell you why it matters; your machines will change the world*
- **To Sophara** he offers action — *you know what is right; I will give you the power to make it so*
- **To Agoran** he offered belonging — *you were empty; here is meaning; here is purpose; here is an enemy that explains your suffering*

The insidious part: he delivers. Luminar's art under his reign *does* have purpose. Agoran *does* have meaning now. It just comes with chains nobody can see.

### The Cycle

His poisoned gifts deepen the isolation in a new way — kingdoms that take his offer close off from the others on his behalf. This feeds him further. He is not just a conqueror. He is a system.

## What He Wants

- A community he controls completely — one that cannot exile him, because he *is* the community
- Forgeholm's technology, bent to his will — direction without wisdom is exactly what a tyrant needs
- Eventually: all five aspects under one crown, and with them, every dimension of human life

## His Army

Misfits, outcasts, and monsters — people who had nothing before he found them. He gave them purpose when the world gave them nothing. They will die for him, and many of them are not bad people. That is the point. His army is not evil, it is *grateful*. Fighting them is not clean.

## Relationship to the Party

He will try to do to the party what he has done to every kingdom: find the wound, offer the cure. He will approach each hero with the thing they most want. He may even be *right* about what they need. He is just the wrong person to give it.

## GM Notes

**His origin:** Where was he exiled from? Was the exile just, unjust, or complicated? If it was unjust, the party has to reckon with that. If it was just, he has to reckon with it.

**The tragic read:** He is solving the same problem the heroes are — he just chose domination instead of cooperation. He saw that the five kingdoms needed each other and decided the solution was one king over all of them, not five equal partners. He is not wrong about the problem. He is catastrophically wrong about the solution.

**The irony of Agoran:** The Kingdom of Community, hollowed out by isolation, became the perfect vessel for him. He didn't corrupt it — it was already empty. He just filled it.

**The question the campaign asks:** Can something that was filled with poison be emptied and refilled? Can Agoran be healed, or just defeated?
