# Ereni Thole, the Warder Who Almost Struck

**Role:** Retired master of the Considered Art; the exception — the philosopher who wants to act; future bearer of Sophara's question
**Location:** The camp on the fork, [the Stoa](../../locations/the-stoa.md) — her wards ring it
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the Sophara arc)*
**Status:** Alive, old, forty years unbelieved — and vindication is walking toward her

## Appearance

Late seventies, chalk-dusted to the wrists, a warder's grey coat gone soft with age. Flexes her right hand when she thinks — the hand it was taken from — the way other people drum their fingers. Her ward-lines are still the cleanest anyone in the Stoa has ever seen. She draws them faster than students a quarter her age, and signs them with a strike-glyph she has no license to use and dares anyone to mention.

## Personality

Precise, dry, and furious in a banked, patient way — the fury of someone who has been *politely* disbelieved for four decades, which is worse than being called a liar. Suffers neither fools nor consolation. Underneath: a teacher to the bone, and the loneliness of the only person in a kingdom who knows the disease is real.

## Backstory

Forty years ago, on the Fens causeway, one of the fog-things that imitate voices had her best student. Ereni shaped a strike — masterwork, textbook, the finest working of her life — and at the instant of release it was *taken*. Not failed: taken, like a word snatched off the tongue, like a hand closing over hers mid-swing. The fog closed. The student was gone.

The schools ruled it the known truth: decisive release is philosophically incoherent; the Considered Art does not strike ([Magic — the Considered Art](../../world/magic.md#sophara--the-considered-art)). Ereni resigned her chair and spent the next forty years compiling **the Thole Catalogue**: three hundred interviews, every attempted strike in living memory — and every one failing at the same *instant*, regardless of school, method, or intent. Failures of philosophy should scatter. Hers cluster like tooth-marks. The schools filed the Catalogue under grief.

When Tam's barges landed the camp on the island, she walked out of retirement, warded it for nothing, and has not left since.

## Relationship to the Party

The party's first believer — and the first person in Sophara who will say aloud what they're circling: *it isn't philosophy. Something is taking it.* She has the Catalogue, the craft, and the standing (however tattered) to put evidence under the party's claims. What she needs is what she's never had: someone who doesn't think she's mad, and a way to make the theft *visible*.

## Secrets

- The Catalogue's sharpest finding, which she has told no one: the failures cluster to the **instant**, not the intent — proof of an external mechanism, if anyone would read it as evidence instead of mourning.
- Twenty years ago, at the last fair, she quietly attempted a small strike on the fairgrounds — and it *came out*. She told no one, because a single anomaly is not data, and because she wept for an hour and could not have said why. (It was the Loosening. She has been waiting twenty years for the fair to come back.)
- Her wards around the camp hold better than any she has ever cast, and she doesn't yet know why *(mixed aspects: Agoran hearth-life braided into Sopharan wardcraft — the graft, working)*.

## GM Notes

**Her vindication is a scene, not a line.** Give it staging: the Catalogue laid open next to the party's evidence; the river rippling; the moment she realizes the camp she's been warding is sitting on the thing that robbed her. Play it as the anchors file plays every reveal — symptom first, mechanism after — and let her be *angry* before she's glad.

**She bears Sophara's question.** The Mother Engine's re-tuning needs "Sophara's question asked inside the forge" ([Forgeholm healing](../../world/kingdoms/forgeholm.md#gm-notes)) — Ereni is its natural bearer: a warder who has spent her whole life asking *should we?* about her own art. Seed her willingness to travel; the woman who hasn't left the Stoa in decades agreeing to walk to Kaldrum is its own beat.

**The dam-break option.** The anchors file promises: one act of resolve and the dam breaks. The strongest candidate on the board is Ereni — the first Sopharan strike in 213 years, cast in the camp's defense, at the Verdict, while the fair sings. If you use it, the strike is the argument: no syllogism in the Hall will do what forty years of stolen resolve arriving all at once does. Decide beforehand what it costs her; a working held that long should not come out free.
