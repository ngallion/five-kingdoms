# Marlow Pitch, the Patron

**Role:** Korrven's agent in Luminar — Tier 2 lieutenant; impresario of the Northbound and holder of the Luminary's debts
**Location:** A gallery-house on the Bill, [Prismere](../../locations/prismere.md)
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the Luminar arc)*
**Status:** Embedded three years; his finale is written, delivered, and waiting

## Appearance

Fifties, immaculate, dressed a careful half-step behind fashion — memorable for warmth, never for style, which in Prismere is camouflage. Attends every debut on the Bill and some in the Flats nobody else would cross water for. His one habit people mention: he *takes no notes* and forgets nothing — the name of your first work, the color you abandoned, the collaborator who left you. Being remembered by Marlow Pitch, artists say, is like standing in sunlight. That is the design.

## Personality

Patient, cultured, and genuinely moved by art — his taste is real, his praise is accurate, and his gratitude to the king is the engine under all of it. Pitch is one of Korrven's grateful ([His Army](the-king.md#his-army)): a man whose own gift curdled young, who spent twenty years watching lesser talents take the Gasp, and whom the king found and gave the only role that ever fit — *the one who sees*. He believes, wholly, that he is saving Luminar's artists from a kingdom that eats them. He is not entirely wrong, which is what makes him dangerous.

He never recruits at the debut. He recruits at **the Morning After** — the crash, when the finished work has gone stale and its maker wakes hollow — arriving with coffee, accurate praise of the corpse, and the observation that it doesn't have to be this way ([the Gleaner](../../world/factions/the-found.md) works the Grey After the same way; it is the same doctrine).

## Backstory

Arrived in 211 AS as a private collector from the Chalklands border with a bottomless purse — Agoran grain-money, laundered through commissions. Three years of flawless patronage later he funds half the Bill, holds paper on most of it, and runs the kingdom's three-part harvest:

- **The Morning After** — his talent-spotters work the crash, offering purpose to the freshly hollow.
- **The Commission of the Lasting** — the Northbound pipeline: Luminar's best, shipped to Commonhold, where their work for the king's spectacles *does* mean something and *does* last ([the offer delivers](the-king.md); the letters home are glowing, and written for them). The Bill's darkening studios are his ledger made visible.
- **The Finale** — the masterstroke: Vane's debts bought quietly over two years, then forgiven, then the manuscript. He did not write it alone *(see Secrets)*.

His standing orders mirror Maro Tallis's in kind: don't break Luminar — *harvest* it, and ready the invitation. The kingdom must ask the king in.

## Relationship to the Party

Delightful. He will know the party's names before they know his, attend anything they do publicly, and praise it with unnerving precision. PCs with an artist's soul — or an artist's wound — get the full treatment: he is Korrven's method retail, the exact right offer at the exact low moment. He does the party genuine favors for as long as they raise the Season's temperature, because a city in ferment debuts harder, crashes harder, and harvests easier.

## Secrets

- **The finale's other author.** The manuscript's spine came north from Commonhold with pages in a hand Pitch doesn't know and doesn't ask about — passages that read like someone who has *seen* a drowned city rise. Whether that hand is a hollowed Northbound artist writing to order, or something Vael showed the king, is yours to decide; either way, Pitch's masterpiece is not entirely his, and it bothers him more than he admits.
- **Completion as a knife.** Artists who refuse him and matter enough find their beloved unfinished works *finished* overnight — a hired hand, a final stroke, and by week's end the piece and its maker's standing are drained and forgotten. It is murder with no body and no law against it. He has done it four times. Old Cantrel's *The Long Morning* ([Prismere](../../locations/prismere.md#people-here)) is on the list if Cantrel keeps talking to the Encore.
- **The Fens escort racket runs through his books.** The King's Wardens on the coast road bill their upkeep through his gallery-house accounts — the paper thread that ties the beloved escorts to Agoran money, and the nearest the party can get to [the Dousers](../../world/the-hollowing.md) from Luminar's side.
- **Vess Tallow is his own wound.** His file on her is the thickest he keeps, and his courtship of her long ago stopped being strategy: she is the artist he never became, doing the thing the king only *promises* — making work that lasts. Some nights he knows that if her method spreads, the offer he serves is obsolete. He has not put this in any report.

His "combat" is the calendar: every session unexposed, another studio goes dark and the finale gets closer.)*

## GM Notes

**The unmasking must beat the applause.** Pitch's position is perfectly legal and genuinely beloved — patron of the Season, forgiver of debts, friend of the escorts keeping the coast road safe. Exposing him is not a matter of proof but of *story*: the Northbound ledger (Dree Fanshaw's playbills, the identical letters, the four finished-and-forgotten works) must be told to the Season's own crowd better than he tells his. The Encore's Recitals are the stage for it; a party that turns Luminar's love of a good reveal against him wins the city in one night. Done clumsily, he simply produces the receipts of his generosity — all real — and the party plays the villains who attacked the arts.

**The finale clock.** If the party never engages him, Vane signs by midwinter: exhaustion, not corruption. Warning signs available early: the studios going dark in a pattern; letters home that rhyme; Strake mentioning that the Patron has asked, twice, politely, for a tour below the stage.

**Can he be turned?** Harder than Maro — his gratitude has taste's conviction behind it. But the crack is Vess: he has already seen a truer version of the thing he serves, four feet from him, for two years. If she stays, teaches, and *names him a witness* — the man who saw her when the Bill wouldn't — the campaign's method cuts its way back in. A turned Pitch delivers the entire Northbound pipeline, the Warden accounts, and the king's iconography plans. Korrven will send the Gleaner's people for him within the season, and he knows it — sheltering a defected Patron is a quest in itself.
