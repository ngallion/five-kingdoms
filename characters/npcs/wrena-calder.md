# Wrena Calder

**Role:** Ally — struck-off mender; keeper of [the Second Stamp](../../world/factions/the-second-stamp.md)
**Location:** A steam-kitchen workshop in the Chimneys, [Kaldrum](../../locations/kaldrum.md)
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the opening arc)*
**Status:** Alive, watched by the Guild, busier than ever

## Appearance

A short, wide-built woman in her fifties, wind-burned from a life on the surface terraces, forever wearing a tool-apron with more pockets than the Guild would certify. Her forearms show the pale ghosts where journeyman's marks were **struck** — burned flat, the Guild's punishment brand of erasure. She has never covered them. In the Chimneys, the scars are read the way medals are.

## Personality

Flinty, funny, and allergic to speeches — she distrusts anyone whose answer to a broken pump is a meeting. Her one visible ritual: before starting any repair, she names aloud who it's for. *"This valve for the Ninth Steam-Kitchen. This brace for Odda's lift-crew."* She learned the habit from the old master who trained her and could not tell you that it is the ghost of [Ferrun's real worship](../../world/religion.md#ferrun-the-shaping-hand-forgeholm--technology) — the purpose spoken aloud over the finished work. She only knows repairs feel wrong without it.

What she fears: not the Guild. She fears the day the Chimneys stop fixing things for each other — because she has watched [the Engine](../../world/anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine) grind the *why* out of better people than her, one shift at a time.

## Backstory

A certified journeyman on the ventilation benches until nine years ago, when a Chimneys air-pump failed in a hard freeze and the work-order sat unsigned in a Guild queue. She repaired it without certification, in the night, for free. People lived. She was struck from the rolls for it — *tampering*, the same word they used for Herrek, though she didn't know that then and would spit when told.

The Chimneys never let her starve. She mended, taught, and organized quietly, and somewhere in those nine years the scattered benches of menders who ink the blank stamp became a fellowship with her at its center. She did not found the Second Stamp. She is simply the one everyone brings the broken things to — machines and people both.

## Relationship to the Party

The party's door into the real Kaldrum — the one below the Bench's sightlines. She'll size them up by watching how they treat the Unmarked, not what they say, and warm fast to anyone who fixes something without being paid for it. Once she trusts them: safehouses, vent-gallery routes, the Chimneys' eyes on Krane's caravans, and blunt truth about which masters can be reached.

## Secrets

- She runs the **Ledger of the Retired** — the Second Stamp's hidden record of every "quietly retired" wright and where they were smuggled. Nine years ago her benches passed a retired senior named [**Ondrel Vasht**](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#key-members) to the Wayfair. She's the one person in Kaldrum who can put the party in a room with him fast.
- Her spoken consecrations *almost take*. Wrights swear her repairs hold past their tolerances; she calls it careful work. *(GM: it's the old braided runework straining against the lattice — see GM Notes.)*
- She knows the vent-galleries the Guild forgot it built, including the ones that pass within sound of [the Root](../../locations/kaldrum.md#gm-notes).
- She remembers the name *Herrek* from her apprenticeship — old wrights' shorthand for "the Guild eats its own" — and knows the tribunal was sealed, though not what's in it.

## GM Notes

**She is the native seed of the healing — but not the whole cure.** The Second Stamp already asks *for whom*; what Forgeholm's re-tuning still needs is imported: Sophara's *should we* and Agoran's answer to *for whom*, physically present at the Mother Engine ([re-tuning needs](../../world/anchors-and-engines.md#forgeholm--the-first-heart-the-mother-engine)). Wrena's role is to be the local practice the imports graft onto — and the proof, standing in front of the Bench, that the *why* survived in the city the Engine was starving it out of.

**Her Loosening moment.** Put her on the Cinderfield during the fair. When she speaks a consecration inside the Loosening, it **takes** — visibly, unmistakably, the purpose binding into the metal the way [no wright has managed in two centuries](../../world/magic.md#forgeholm--runework). She will stand very still, and the party has just watched the campaign's magic-reveal chain begin in a middle-aged mender's hands. If a PC braids with her (their aspect, her craft), grant the Hope per [the Braid](../../world/magic.md#at-the-daggerheart-table).

**The mirror of Krane.** Same terrace, same crime, opposite roads — see [Krane's GM notes](ilsabet-krane.md#gm-notes). When Krane offers the Stamp marks for the Unmarked, Wrena's refusal must not be smug or easy: it should cost her benches, maybe friends. Her counter-argument is not ideology; it's the named repair — *a mark he gives you binds to him. The work I sign binds to the Ninth Steam-Kitchen.* Whether that argument holds the Stamp together is a live question the party can tip.

**If Kaldrum falls,** she does not leave. The Second Stamp goes under-terrace and becomes the occupation's resistance with her at its center — and the liberation arc's first contact.
