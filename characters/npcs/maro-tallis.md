# Maro Tallis

**Role:** Korrven's agent in Sophara — Tier 2 lieutenant; organizer of [the Closed Hand](../../world/factions/the-closed-hand.md)
**Location:** Sponsored scholar's rooms, West Bank, [the Stoa](../../locations/the-stoa.md)
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the Sophara arc)*
**Status:** Embedded four years; his cover has just become mortally dangerous

## Appearance

Late thirties, ordinary on purpose — the kind of face you find yourself agreeing with. Dresses like a scholar who hasn't forgotten how to work. Wears a mourning-knot at his wrist in the colors of a Commonhold district he has never lived in, and touches it, gently, at exactly the right moments.

## Personality

Patient, competent, and genuinely warm — because his gratitude is *real*. Maro is not a mercenary wearing a cause; he is one of Korrven's grateful ([His Army](the-king.md#his-army)): a man who had nothing, whom the king gave purpose, and who believes to his marrow that he is saving Sophara from the fate of standing alone. He never argues first. He funds, he organizes, he fetches chairs — and at the exact moment of a room's frustration, he supplies the phrase: *the Colloquy will never move. The offer is just strength on loan. Since the council cannot act — someone must.*

## Backstory

The truth: a warden of Agoran's interior, one of the quiet corps that keeps the king's story clean — chosen for this posting precisely because his loneliness before Korrven was real and his conversion total. Arrived in 210 AS as "an exile of the '09 flight," with a flawless sad story from Commonhold's weaver-quarter, and spent four years becoming the most reliable man in the Stoa: the one who books the courtyard, keeps the accounts, remembers everyone's name.

His standing orders were subtle — keep Sophara undecided, or deciding *toward* the king; the paralysis serves, so never simply break things. The camp changed everything. New orders have come north: **map the pipeline.** Somewhere among the granges' barge-runs and fair-colors is the route that keeps bleeding Agoran, and his masters want it plugged. Names have started traveling north in the grain-ledgers.

## Relationship to the Party

Helpful. *So* helpful. The first to welcome them into the Hand, the first to praise their deeds in the right courtyards, the first to know their lodgings, their habits, their wounds. He will do the party genuine good for as long as their success radicalizes the movement in his direction — and he is Korrven's method in miniature: what he offers is real, and poisoned.

## Secrets

- His reports go north folded into the granges' grain-ledgers via a bought clerk on the Girdle post — the same barge-quays Tam's runs use. The pipeline and the leak share a dock.
- **The camp is his nightmare.** Hearthmother Bry's people include a weaver who lived three streets from his claimed home. He has stopped going to the island, and the avoidance is a tell — the '09 exile who won't visit the '09 exiles.
- His endgame motion, drafted and waiting: that Sophara accept the king's protection *provisionally, pending the Colloquy's review* — the fist, in Sophara's own handwriting.
- He was recruited out of the Thornfell camps. There was a season, before the king found him, when he nearly walked to the Wayfair's fires instead ([the mirror](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#gm-notes)). He does not think about it.

## Daggerheart Stats

*(Tier 2 adversary — social. He should never be the party's match with a blade; he is their match in a room. Move ideas: **Turn the Phrase** — a PC's own argument comes back radicalized from an NPC's mouth a scene later; **The Reasonable Motion** — under Fear, a crowd or council shifts one step toward his position; **Names Travel North** — mark a Fear: something the party said near him reaches Agoran. His "combat" is the arc: every session he's unexposed, the Hand tips further toward the fist.)*

## GM Notes

**The unmasking is a landmine, not a victory lap.** Expose him publicly and every real refugee in the camp pays for it — *the exiles sent a spy* is exactly the story the orthodoxy's worst voices want, and blowback lands on Bry's hearths within days. The clean unmasking needs the camp's own testimony front and center (the weaver who knew his streets is the thread), and Bry deciding how the camp meets the news defines whether it survives the arc. Give the party the choice between the fast reveal and the safe one, and make the difference visible.

**The ambush clock.** If the party never smells him, his mapping completes: a run of Tam's is taken on the Fens causeway mid-arc — lanterns in the fog, and the fog answering back — and the camp is blamed for its own tragedy. First warning signs available earlier: the bought clerk spends too freely; Bry mentions the man who never visits; a grain-ledger page with too many names in the margin.

**Can he be turned?** His gratitude is real, which means it can, in principle, be re-aimed — the party offering him a truer belonging is the campaign's whole question asked at Tier 2, and the Wayfair's fires are the life he almost chose. It should be *hard*: harder than exposing him, harder than killing him. But a turned Maro — the king's own proof that the method cuts both ways — is worth more than both, and Korrven will feel it like a re-tuned anchor.
