# The Gleaner

**Role:** The Found's chief recruiter and the Quiet Harvest's selector — Tier 2 adversary shadowing the Wayfair's circuit
**Location:** Wherever the fair just was — she works the Grey After, one town behind the wagons
**First appeared:** *(not yet — seeded from Session 1's fair onward)*
**Status:** Harvesting

## Appearance

A weathered, comfortable woman with a carrier's pack and a face that belongs wherever it is — you'd swear you'd seen her at the last fair, and you had. Dresses in road-grey with one incongruous touch: a scrap of festival ribbon at her wrist, old and sun-bleached. Carries a ledger wrapped in oilcloth. When she thinks nobody can hear her, she hums the fair's lullaby — accurately, beautifully, and always stopping one line short of where the missing verse would be. Not because she doesn't know where it goes. Because she does.

## Personality

Patient, kind, and true — the Gleaner never lies, which is what makes her lethal. She finds people in the fair-ache, when the Loosening's warmth has drained back out of the world, and offers exactly what the Wayfair offered with none of the leaving: a place, a name at a table, work that matters, *no one stands alone*. Every word accurate. She is Korrven's method walking: the wound found, the cure offered, the chains invisible — and she believes it all, because it worked on her first.

## Backstory

*(Invented for the Harvest — rename or reshape freely.)* She was born to the fair. Her name was **Lark**, and she was one of Sera Kindlow's own troupe until, some fifteen years ago, a Grey After came that never lifted: the Loosening simply stopped touching her. She stood in the middle of the world's warmest crowds and felt nothing at all — the loneliest condition a human being has ever invented — and the fair, which sees every wound but its own, never noticed. Korrven noticed. His people found her in a rim-town winter, and what he offered her was not belonging but *usefulness*: if the warmth won't have you, be the one who gathers for it.

She has worked the circuit's wake ever since — the one person alive who knows the fair's rhythms from inside and owes them nothing.

## Relationship to the Party

They will have seen her — at the fairs, in the crowds, unremarkable — long before they see her. She recruits ahead of them and behind them, and if a PC carries a visible wound of the belonging kind, she will eventually make them the offer too, sincerely and without pressure. She does not fight and does not flee; cornered, she simply tells the truth about what she offers people, and lets the party notice how many of the people she's offered it to are better fed than before she found them.

## Secrets

- **The two-column ledger.** Left column: the aching, offered the Found. Right column: the flagged — those who refuse but matter. Dissenters with followings. Cast-out wrights with craft. Blood that the March will tolerate. The right column feeds [the quiet wagons](../../quests/active/the-quiet-harvest.md); she tells herself it is a waiting list.
- **She flags Wayfarers with special care** — she can pick the fair's blood out of a crowd by how the Grey After sits on them. Sera believes Lark died in that rim-town winter. The scrap of ribbon at her wrist is from the sixth wagon's canopy, and she could not tell you why she keeps it.
- **She has never once gone to the Foundation.** She routes people to Nowhere and has never looked at it. This is the load-bearing wall of her peace, and one honest journey would bring it down.

## GM Notes

**She is the pipeline's human face — play the kindness straight.** Every scene with the Gleaner should be warmer than the party expects and worse in retrospect. The reveal that her charity and the black-bagging are one operation should land on players the way the Wardens/Dousers reveal lands on kingdoms: not *the kind ones were fake*, but *the kindness was the mechanism*.

**The Sera scene.** Lark alive, grey-cloaked, and harvesting the fair's own wake is a knife aimed at the Wayfair's heart — and at its blind spot: the fair that binds every wound but never saw hers. When Sera learns, do not let it be in passing. The Fairkeeper who reads every wound, face to face with the one she missed, is the Wayfair's whole story asked as a question — and how Sera answers it (grief, fury, or the open seat one more time) teaches the party what the fair actually is.

**Her turn runs through the Foundation.** She has never looked at where the right column goes. A party that *makes* her look — brings her Fenn, or brings her to the Line — is detonating her one defended wall. What's on the other side is unwritten: the woman who feels nothing at the Loosening might feel nothing here too, and become something genuinely frightening — or the ledger-keeper might finally read her own ledger. Both endings are true to her. Choose at the table.

**If the party takes the ledger,** the Harvest loses its selector and the takings go blunt — the wagons start taking *wrong* people, loudly, and the pipeline's secrecy begins to fail on its own. Removing the Gleaner doesn't stop the machine; it makes the machine visible. That's a victory with a body count, and worth letting the players discover it is.
