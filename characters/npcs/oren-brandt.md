# Oren Brandt, the Last Moot-Speaker

**Role:** Keeper of the old Agoran — elder of [the Eleventh Chair](../../world/factions/the-eleventh-chair.md); early-game ally and the campaign's living archive
**Location:** The Downsmoot, in the [Chalklands](../../world/geography.md#the-chalklands-luminar--agoran)
**First appeared:** *(not yet — reachable from Tier 1)*
**Status:** Alive, old, and running out of time to be the only one who remembers

## Appearance

Seventies, tall and gone spare, a harvest-wreath beard and drover's hands. Wears the moot-speaker's stole he walked out in — Granary wool, faded from banner-gold to hay. His speaking voice is ruined by age and chalk-dust; his *singing* voice, unaccountably, is not, and hearing it explains everything about what the Ring used to be.

## Personality

Warm the old way — which turns out to be different. Oren's welcome has no roll behind it, no naming-day file, no debt accruing: he feeds who comes, learns your name because he wants it, and forgets to ask your business for days. He is stubborn, funny, unhurried, and carries his grief the way he carries everything, out where guests can see it — he will talk about the Fall, his prentice, and the grace with anyone who sits long enough, because talking is how a moot-speaker keeps things alive. Underneath: the specific loneliness of a man who is a *library*, and knows it, and watches the exile-born children dance steps only he can check.

## Backstory

Thirty years the moot-speaker at Commonhold's Ring — the voice of the harvest-grace, keeper of the naming-days, the man who opened the moot all Agoran gathered for. He watched the tables hollow for decades ([the extraction](../../world/anchors-and-engines.md#agoran--the-warm-heart-now-the-kings-heart)) and stayed at his post, because the words were a trust even when the warmth stopped answering them.

In 209 the king came, and was welcomed, and asked — courteously, reasonably, the whole Ring watching — that the grace name him. Oren stood at the head table and sang the harvest-grace exactly as he had for thirty years, and where the king's name was meant to go, he held **silence** — one full breath, the oldest room in Agoran dead quiet — then finished the verse with the guest's blessing, laid down the speaker's staff on the long table, and walked out through the singing. No one stopped him. The Found tell it as the sad exit of a man too old to change; the exiles date their calendar from it. Both remember the silence.

Five years on, his refusal has become a place: the **Downsmoot**, the ring of turf halls under the six dancers where the exiles hold the world's last true harvest moot. What Oren kept is not a grievance but a *practice* — the grace with the right name, the songs, the steps, the guest-law, taught nightly to children born in chalk country who have never seen the Ring he means to give it all back to.

## Relationship to the Party

The plan's promised early ally: the door into everything Agoran before the party can survive Agoran itself. He can teach them what the kingdom *was* — which is precisely the re-tuning stock the campaign needs ([Agoran's aspect, required at two other Hearts](../../world/factions/the-eleventh-chair.md#relationship-to-the-party)) — hand them the drovers' safe paths, the chalk-wall map of the safe-roads crack, and letters that open Chair hearths in five kingdoms. What he asks of them grows with trust: carry a verse to a scattered hearth; find out if his name still stands on any roll in Commonhold *(it does — see [Iveth Kall](iveth-kall.md#secrets), though he doesn't know it)*; and, someday, get an old man to the Ring one more time.

## Secrets

- **He remembers the whole grace.** The harvest-grace has an archaic closing couplet — thanks given to *the guest who set the table before we came* — that had dropped out of use generations before the Fall as meaningless. Oren keeps it in because his own teacher did. It is Agoran's [Guest custom](../../world/religion.md#the-guest-customs) surviving in liturgy: the couplet is *Amara's*, and a Wayfair elder hearing Oren sing it entire should go quiet the way they do around Splendor healers ([the chain](../../world/religion.md#gm-notes)). Oren knows only that the old words feel weight-bearing, like a beam you don't cut.
- **The staff is still on the table.** By Ring custom the moot-speaker's staff passes hand to hand, never lifted by its own last holder. It has lain where he laid it for five years — Korrven, showman enough to know a relic when he sees one, has pointedly left it untouched: *the seat is open; the old man may return whenever he accepts the grace.* A standing, patient, poisoned invitation. Oren knows about it, and what it costs him to know is considerable.
- **He is the clock.** The songs are taught, but the *corpus* — forty years of graces, naming-rites, moot-law, the couplet — lives whole in one aging memory. He has begun forgetting small things and telling no one. The Chair's real emergency is not the king; it is time. A party with a scholar, a songwright, or simple patience can do the campaign's quietest great deed: get the old Agoran out of one skull and into many *(a gathering-quest shape — see GM Notes)*.

## GM Notes

**Use him early.** The plan calls the exiles "great early-game allies and lore sources," and Oren is the payoff: reachable in Tier 1 via the Chalklands leg, Tam's people, or any Chair hearth, safely distant from the campaign's sharp end, and able to seed nearly every long thread over one supper — the Fall as the fallen saw it, the safe-roads question, the guest-law, the couplet. The party should love the old Agoran before they ever fight for it; Oren is how.

**The gathering-quest.** *Gather the moot:* travel the hearths recording the corpus before it fades — verses from scattered elders, steps from the fork camp, the couplet from Oren alone. It is a low-combat, high-lore quest shape that doubles as the campaign's ammunition run: everything gathered is re-tuning material, Guest-custom evidence, and (assembled) the counter-liturgy for the climax. If the [Concord Tongue thread](../../world/history.md#gm-notes) is in play, a grace recovered by collaboration with the erased *sticks* — cross-reference the readings.

**The return to the Ring.** The liberation's centerpiece ([Commonhold — GM Notes](../../locations/commonhold.md#gm-notes)): Oren walking back in as a *guest*, the eleventh place laid at the head table, the staff still where he left it, and the old grace sung against the king's — with [Iveth](iveth-kall.md#gm-notes), his own prentice, deciding which one the city joins. Korrven's counter is the standing invitation: he will offer Oren the staff, the seat, the moot restored — everything but the name in the grace — and mean every word. Oren held one silence against him already; whether he can hold it again, in front of a Ring full of people singing the wrong grace *warmly*, is the scene.

**If he dies before the return** — and at his age, the clock is honest — the corpus is whatever the party gathered, the couplet lives or dies by whether anyone learned it, and the return still happens: someone else walks in as the guest, carrying his verses. The Chair's law was never *the speaker*; it was *the table*. A campaign where a PC sings the old grace at the Ring because Oren taught them is not the failure branch. It might be the best one.
