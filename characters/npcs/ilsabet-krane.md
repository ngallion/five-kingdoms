# Ilsabet Krane, the King's Provisioner

**Role:** Villain — Korrven's emissary in Forgeholm; Tier 2 lieutenant
**Location:** Guest quarters on the Bench, [Kaldrum](../../locations/kaldrum.md) — with a grain-caravan camp outside the Girdle-gate
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the opening arc)*
**Status:** Alive, operating openly, weeks into her work

## Appearance

Late thirties, Chimneys-pale, dressed in immaculate wright's leathers that bear no Guild colors — and on her forearms, **wright's marks the Guild never granted**, in a design of her own devising. Every master who sees them understands the provocation instantly; that is what they are for. Wears a signet ring cut with the Agoran grain-sheaf, and uses it to seal everything, including notes that don't need sealing.

## Personality

Warm, unhurried, and terrifyingly well-prepared. Krane never lies where the truth will serve, never threatens, and **always delivers** — the grain arrives early, the contract terms are honored to the letter, the promised orders are real. She has Korrven's gift in miniature: she listens for what a person is starved of and offers exactly that, genuinely, with the poison built into the delivery mechanism rather than the gift ([his method](the-king.md#he-knows-the-wound)). She is not a believer performing loyalty. She is what gratitude looks like when it is total.

## Backstory

Born Unmarked in the Chimneys. Brilliant, obvious to everyone, and unsponsorable — no line, no family marks, no path to apprenticeship under Guild law. She repaired machines illegally through her teens, was struck from the labor rolls for it, and walked out through the [Thornfell](../../world/geography.md#the-thornfell-agoran--forgeholm) at nineteen, where Korrven's camps found her half-frozen. He did not recruit her. He looked at her repairs, asked her what she would have built if they'd let her, listened to the whole answer, and then had her teach it to others. The marks on her arms were his idea: *design your own — the Guild's dies were never going to fit you.*

She was with him before Agoran fell. She would die for him without noticing the question.

## Relationship to the Party

Courteous, curious, and immediately attentive — a party of mixed kingdoms is exactly the sort of remarkable, unshelved people her king collects. She will make each PC a real offer before the arc is out ([he will approach each hero with the thing they most want](the-king.md#relationship-to-the-party) — she is the advance version). She does not consider the party enemies. She considers them *early*.

## Secrets

- **The Compact's true payload:** the Agoran Compact — a colossal standing order of rail, engines, and arms that gives Forgeholm's forges direction for a generation — is drafted so that "output standardization" moves Guild certification, clause by renewing clause, under the king's provisioning authority, and ends with his inspectors at [the Root](../../locations/kaldrum.md#gm-notes). Nothing in it is false. All of it is a door.
- **The standing order:** find the sealed record of the **Tamperer's Tribunal, 195 AS**, and bring it to the king unread. Korrven wants his father's condemnation in his own hands when he takes the city — vindication is half of why he's coming home. Her clerks are already asking the archive about "precedents from the nineties."
- She knows the king was exiled from this city — an intimacy he shared with her, exile to exile. She does **not** know whose son he is or what Herrek actually did; nobody living knows that.
- After the stutter, her instructions are pre-written: blame the fair, offer Guild protection, and make Forgeholm ask *him* for help. The frame-up was drafted before the fair arrived — the stutter merely made it easy.
- She coordinates **the Gleaner** ([the recruiter shadowing the fair](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#campaign-hooks)) whenever the circuit is in reach of her operation; the Grey After outside the walls is being harvested while she works the Bench inside them.

## GM Notes

**She is the argument made flesh.** Krane is what Korrven's offer looks like when it *works*: the Guild's law threw her away, and the king built her into someone extraordinary. The party should notice — before the arc ends — that she is right about everything except him. Every unjust thing about Forgeholm (the marks system, the Unmarked, the scoured lines) is real, and she is its living indictment; the campaign's honesty depends on not flattening her into a schemer.

**The mirror of Wrena Calder.** Same city, same terrace, same crime — unlicensed repair, done for people the Guild wouldn't serve. [Wrena](wrena-calder.md) stayed and built [the Second Stamp](../../world/factions/the-second-stamp.md); Ilsabet left and was built into this. Put them in one scene if you possibly can. Krane will offer the Stamp everything it wants — marks for the Unmarked is *actual Korrven policy* — and mean it, and some benches will go with her ([the faction can split](../../world/factions/the-second-stamp.md#gm-notes)).

**Her lever.** She has one wound left: the marks on her arms are her own design because they *had* to be. If the party — or a converted [Vorn](halda-vorn.md) — ever offers her real certification, the Guild's dies struck true for the girl from the Chimneys, she will refuse it. Watch what it costs her to refuse it. This is the crack Vael would never let Korrven leave open, and the seed of a late-campaign defection if your table wants one lieutenant who can be reached.

**Escalation track.** Weeks 1–2: the Compact courtship (she may already have the Bench's votes). The stutter: the frame-up, protection offer, tribunal push. If unopposed: inspectors at the Root by season's end and the king's homecoming with the gates open ([if the city falls](../../locations/kaldrum.md#gm-notes)). The party's wins are measured in clauses struck, votes flipped, and the tribunal of the fair collapsing in open hall.
