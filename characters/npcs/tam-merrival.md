# Tam Merrival, "the Myriad"

**Role:** The Wayfair's closing headliner; smuggler; the fair's ears — key NPC of the Sophara arc
**Location:** Currently the Meander valley, running the Quiet Circuit; his stage stood empty at the Forgeholm fair
**First appeared:** *(by absence — Session 1, the Forgeholm solstice)*
**Status:** Alive, exhausted, and running out of luck faster than he admits

## Appearance

Fortyish, travel-plain, forgettable — offstage, Tam is nobody in particular, which has saved his life more times than the fair knows. Onstage he is **the Myriad**: five-braided regalia, a voice that fills a fairground without strain, and the only performer alive whom every kingdom's crowds remember by heart between visits — insofar as they remember the fair at all.

## The Act

The Myriad closes every fair, the last act before the lullaby: a multi-sensory extravaganza that comes at the crowd through every door at once — engine-driven stagecraft *(Forgeholm)*, painted light and perfume and tasting-cups passed hand to hand *(Luminar)*, one true and astonishing thing revealed and proven mid-show *(Veritas)*, a question put to the crowd that the crowd must answer — and does, roaring, the only place in the world Sopharans vote *(Sophara)* — and a chorus no one knew they knew, sung by everyone, holding the whole thing together *(Agoran)*.

*(GM: the act is a deliberate five-strand braid — the fair's one law performed as a single working. The Loosening peaks on closing night because the Myriad is the peak; he is the closest thing to a full rite of the old Concord still performed anywhere ([the Wayfair — GM Notes](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#gm-notes)). He half-knows. He'd call it showmanship.)*

## Personality

Two men. The Myriad is enormous — generous, blazing, everyone's. Tam is quiet, watchful, and remembers every name he has ever been told, which is both his craft and his cover: people tell the fair's headliner *everything*, in every kingdom, and Tam is why the Wayfair carries "the only news that still moves between kingdoms." He is Sera Kindlow's unspoken intelligencer — nothing so formal as a spy, nothing so innocent as a gossip — and his one law within the law: what he hears is used to *bind*, never to harm.

## Backstory

Agoran-born. He was on circuit when Agoran fell in 209 — came home to a kingdom that smiled wrong and a family already speaking the king's grace at table. He left through the gate he'd entered, and went back the next year under the fair's colors, and led eleven people out.

The **Quiet Circuit** grew from that: exiles dressed as troupe — the kingdoms' memory-blur slides off anyone wearing the fair's colors, and Tam has learned exactly how far that grace stretches *(GM: the ward-slip extends to whomever the fair claims as its own — [why the borders open](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#gm-notes))* — then south by grange-barge down the Meander, with Ambel Tors's farmers playing dumb at every lock. Two hundred souls in five years. This summer's run — families fleeing the purges before the Forgeholm mobilization — was the largest ever, and the reason his stage stood dark at Kaldrum. He chose the landing himself: **the island at the fork**, because no writ can clear it. He put the camp on the one ground in Sophara where the law must finally answer.

## Relationship to the Party

They know his absence before his face — the dark stage at the Session 1 fair, Sera's thin "resting his voice." In Sophara he is the crisis's quartermaster: the camp, the barge-routes, the granges' trust, and five kingdoms' worth of quiet intelligence. What he cannot do is what he needs the party for — argue in the Hall, fight in the fog, and find the leak he can feel but not see.

## Secrets

- **The Gleaner has his scent.** A recruiter working the Grey After has been asking, fair after fair, about "the one who's never there" ([the Wayfair — Ongoing hooks](../../world/factions/the-wayfair.md#campaign-hooks)).
- The pipeline and Maro's leak share the barge-quays — Tam has noticed the granges' ledgers passing through one clerk too many, and hasn't yet had a week free to pull the thread.
- He is tired in the way that gets people killed, and the Myriad's shows are starting to cost him more than they used to.

## GM Notes

**The return.** Tam comes home *with* the midwinter fair to the Stoa — the Myriad's first show in a year, on the Stub, the night before the Verdict. Meren knows exactly what his show does to a Sopharan crowd and must decide whether to permit it; that hearing is worth playing. The show itself is the arc's engine-room: stage it, let the crowd answer his question with the Verdict on every mind, and let the party feel the window the Loosening opens.

**The ambush thread.** If Maro goes unexposed, the run taken on the Fens causeway is *Tam's* run — he insists on going back out, and the party either rides with him or hears about it. Do not let this beat happen offscreen.

**If he dies,** the Quiet Circuit needs new hands — and the strongest candidates are standing at the table. A campaign where the party inherits the pipeline is a campaign where the fair's work becomes theirs; grief as recruitment. Use sparingly, and only after the party loves him.
