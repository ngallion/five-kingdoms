# Casta Meren, Prolocutor of the Colloquy

**Role:** Ruler of Sophara (insofar as it has one) — keeper of the agenda; the party's opponent in argument, then hinge
**Location:** The Colloquy Hall, [the Stoa](../../locations/the-stoa.md)
**First appeared:** *(not yet — planned for the Sophara arc)*
**Status:** Alive, presiding over nothing, ruling everything that matters

## Appearance

A small, straight-backed woman in her seventies, in scholar's grey without ornament except the Prolocutor's ring — a scale at rest, worn facing inward. Speaks slowly and is never interrupted; not because any rule forbids it, but because nobody wants to miss the end of the sentence. Her marginalia are famous; her praise is rarer than verdicts.

## Personality

Meren is the finest weigher alive, and she is *kind* — that's what makes her dangerous. She believes, wholly and beautifully, that a closed question is a dead one: that the open question is the living mind of a civilization, and a verdict is where thought goes to stop. And she has the argument the party actually has to beat: **Agoran decided.** Decision is the door tyrants enter by; the king's whole method is to offer people the power to enact their answers. A kingdom that concludes nothing can be seduced by no one. Sophara's paralysis, in her weighing, is not a wound. It is armor.

She is wrong — the paralysis is extraction, not protection, and the anesthesia is the theft ([the Still Heart](../../world/anchors-and-engines.md#sophara--the-still-heart)) — but she cannot be *shouted* wrong. She can only be out-weighed.

## Backstory

Elected Prolocutor at thirty-four, the youngest ever — because young Casta Meren was the radical who swore to convene the Colloquy. She spent six years untangling the quorum knot and came within two schools of a sitting; the Provisional broke it quietly, procedurally, and without fingerprints. She has never once spoken of it. The philosophy she built afterward — the reverence for the open question — is a cathedral raised over a defeat, and she has lived in it so long she has forgotten it has a foundation.

Forty years on, she has read more of the Pending than any person alive, keeps the agenda flawlessly for a council that never meets, and renews the Bell-rope warrant every year in her own hand.

## Relationship to the Party

The gatekeeper of the Verdict, the docket, and the Hall. She will grant the party a formal hearing — procedure demands it, and she honors procedure the way Vorn honors measurement — and then she will take their argument apart with genuine regret and absolute precision. She is fair to a fault. That fault is the door.

## Secrets

- The broken quorum of her youth — and that First Steward Verch, then a junior clerk, drafted the procedural motion that broke it. She has never checked whose hand wrote it. She has never let herself.
- She has read the Enforcement Conjecture — the schools' deduction that the isolation is *imposed* — and privately finds it persuasive. It is filed, in her own priority marks, near the top of the Pending. Pending.
- She knows exactly what the Myriad's show does to a Sopharan crowd, from the last fair, twenty years ago. When the Wayfair petitions to raise its fair on the Stub, she must decide whether to permit the one thing that makes her people able to act — and she will notice that she is deciding.

## GM Notes

**Her hinge is the Finding.** The Deferral waits on one question: *what happened?* Meren has spent her life honoring that question. Bring her the true history of the Sundering — evidenced, braided from Veritan record and Wayfair testimony and what the party has seen with their own eyes — and her own method does the rest: the finest weigher alive, given true weights at last. Her conversion scene is the mirror of [Vorn's](halda-vorn.md#gm-notes) *bring me a number*: **bring her the finding.** Flipped, she doesn't just allow the Verdict — she *prosecutes* it, and the voice that kept every question open for forty years closing the oldest one should be the loudest sound in the arc except the Bell.

**How Maro works her: he doesn't.** He needs her exactly where she is — immovable, eloquent, blocking the docket — until frustration ripens the movement for *since the Colloquy cannot act, someone must*. Her orthodoxy is his cover. If she starts to move early, watch him quietly switch to defending her right to deliberate.

**If she breaks the wrong way:** she endorses the motion to accept the king's protection *provisionally, pending review* — sincerely, as the option that closes nothing forever. The deadliest word in Sophara is Meren saying "provisionally." Keep her salvageable to the end: even under the king's peace, a Finding is a Finding.
